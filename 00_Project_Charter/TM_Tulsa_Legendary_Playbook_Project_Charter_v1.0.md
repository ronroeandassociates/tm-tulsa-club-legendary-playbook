## Purpose

Create a comprehensive, durable, and reusable operating playbook for Tulsa Club Toastmasters that captures the club’s best practices, standard operating procedures, annual rhythms, officer duties, meeting roles, event systems, hospitality standards, guest conversion model, and leadership transition processes.

The Legendary Playbook is intended to function as the club’s institutional operating manual so that club quality does not depend on memory, personality, or informal tradition alone. It will preserve proven practices, reduce leadership drift, improve consistency, and make officer transition materially easier.

## Objective

Develop a complete markdown-based playbook that documents how Tulsa Club Toastmasters operates at a high standard across the full club year, with clear procedures for weekly meetings, officers, meeting roles, annual events, hospitality, guest experience, elections, and leadership transition.

The finished playbook should allow a new officer team to assume responsibility for the club with a practical, structured reference that enables continuity, quality control, and growth.

## Strategic Rationale

Tulsa Club Toastmasters has developed a substantial body of practical operating knowledge regarding:

- weekly meeting timing and structure
- guest integration and conversion
- room layout within venue constraints
- use of table-based participation systems
- annual event architecture
- officer elections and induction timing
- hospitality and food planning
- club contest planning
- anniversary and Christmas traditions
- leadership showcase structure
- officer transition discipline

Without a formal playbook, this knowledge remains vulnerable to loss during officer turnover. This project converts operational insight into durable institutional doctrine.

## Project Principles

The playbook should be built according to the following principles:

### 1. Practicality Over Theory
Every section must help someone run the club better in real conditions.

### 2. Reusability
Procedures should be written so they can be repeated annually or weekly.

### 3. Clarity
Instructions should be direct, structured, and easy to execute.

### 4. Institutional Continuity
The document should preserve club knowledge beyond individual officers.

### 5. High Hospitality
Guest experience should be treated as a strategic system, not an afterthought.

### 6. Operational Excellence
The club should function like a disciplined professional organization, not an ad hoc gathering.

## Assumptions

This charter assumes the following operating realities:

- Tulsa Club meets on Mondays
- the operating year runs from July 1 through June 30
- the club does not meet on federal holidays that fall on Mondays
- the club takes the last two weeks of December off
- the venue uses square tables with four chairs at each table
- the club prefers to avoid rearranging the room extensively each week
- the club intends to use markdown as the primary drafting format for this phase
- the club has recurring annual anchor events that should be formalized
- officer transitions benefit from written operational doctrine

## Constraints

The project is subject to the following constraints:

- current phase limited to `.md` deliverables
- playbook must reflect actual Tulsa Club venue and scheduling realities
- procedures should remain manageable for volunteer officers
- content must be sufficiently detailed to be useful but not so over-engineered that the club will ignore it
- the structure must remain compatible with future refinement and archival

## Risks

### Risk 1: Over-Complexity
The playbook could become so large or formal that officers do not use it.

**Mitigation:** write procedures in a direct, checklist-oriented manner.

### Risk 2: Incomplete Institutional Capture
Important traditions or operational knowledge may remain undocumented.

**Mitigation:** build the playbook section by section and review for gaps.

### Risk 3: Officer Drift
Future officers may ignore the documented system.

**Mitigation:** make the playbook practical, concise where possible, and visibly helpful.

### Risk 4: Stale Documentation
The playbook may become outdated over time.

**Mitigation:** include annual review and revision expectations.

### Risk 5: Misalignment with Actual Club Practice
The playbook could document an idealized club rather than the real one.

**Mitigation:** ground every section in existing Tulsa Club practices and agreed best practices.
