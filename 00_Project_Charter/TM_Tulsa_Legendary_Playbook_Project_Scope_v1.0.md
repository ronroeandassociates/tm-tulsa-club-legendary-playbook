# Scope Statement

This project defines the scope for developing the **Tulsa Club Toastmasters Legendary Playbook**, a structured operational guide documenting the systems, procedures, and best practices that enable the club to operate consistently, professionally, and sustainably across leadership transitions.

The playbook will document how the club conducts:

- weekly meetings
- officer operations
- guest integration
- hospitality and event hosting
- contests and special events
- annual leadership transitions
- club traditions and recurring programs

The project converts informal knowledge and evolving best practices into **structured institutional documentation**.

---

# In Scope

The following domains are included in the Legendary Playbook.

---

## 1. Club Identity and Culture

Documentation of the Tulsa Club’s guiding philosophy and operational mindset.

Includes:

- mission and purpose
- culture expectations
- professionalism standards
- member support philosophy
- guest experience philosophy

Purpose:

Establish a **consistent identity regardless of officer changes**.

---

## 2. Weekly Meeting System

Documentation of the standard meeting architecture used by the club.

Includes:

- meeting timing structure
- agenda structure
- transitions between segments
- guest orientation
- table-based participation system
- pre-meeting and post-meeting expectations

Key parameters documented:

- meeting start time
- meeting duration
- meeting rhythm
- table configuration
- speaking area

---

## 3. Meeting Role Standard Operating Procedures

Detailed SOPs for all major meeting roles.

Roles documented:

- Toastmaster
- Table Topics Master
- General Evaluator
- Timer
- Grammarian
- Ah-Counter
- Speaker
- Evaluator

Each SOP will include:

- purpose
- responsibilities
- preparation expectations
- execution steps
- timing standards
- quality expectations
- common mistakes

---

## 4. Officer Role SOPs

Detailed procedures for elected officer positions.

Positions documented:

- President
- Vice President Education
- Vice President Membership
- Vice President Public Relations
- Secretary
- Treasurer
- Sergeant at Arms

Officer SOPs will include:

- strategic responsibilities
- weekly duties
- monthly duties
- annual responsibilities
- collaboration expectations
- handoff procedures

---

## 5. Annual Event System

Documentation of recurring Tulsa Club events and how they are executed.

Events covered:

- Leadership Showcase
- Officer Induction Ceremony
- September Speech Contest
- January Speech Contest
- Club Anniversary Meeting
- Christmas Party
- Open House / Guest Night

Each event will include:

- planning timeline
- required roles
- promotion expectations
- room setup
- hospitality planning
- meeting flow
- follow-up actions

---

## 6. Guest Experience System

Documentation of the club’s guest pipeline.

Includes:

Guest journey stages:

1. Invitation
2. Arrival
3. Orientation
4. Participation
5. Follow-up
6. Membership invitation

Key topics covered:

- guest ambassador role
- table integration
- follow-up communication
- conversion philosophy

Goal:

Provide a **consistent, welcoming guest experience**.

---

## 7. Hospitality System

Documentation of the club’s approach to food and refreshments during special events.

Includes:

- standard event hospitality packages
- Sam’s Club tray planning
- serving logistics
- setup and cleanup procedures
- budget considerations
- hospitality lead responsibilities

Events requiring hospitality:

- open houses
- contests
- anniversary meeting
- leadership showcase
- Christmas party

---

## 8. Room Layout Standard

Documentation of the room configuration used by Tulsa Club.

Constraints addressed:

- venue tables seat four members
- room should not require extensive rearranging each week
- clear speaking area must exist
- guests must be integrated easily

Includes:

- speaker positioning
- table orientation
- guest sign-in placement
- hospitality placement
- banner placement

---

## 9. Table Participation System

Documentation of the table-based participation strategy used by the club.

Includes:

- table roles
- table topics selection method
- guest integration at tables
- table rotation strategy
- engagement practices

Purpose:

Increase participation and maintain energy in meetings using **small group dynamics**.

---

## 10. Annual Operating Calendar

Documentation of the club’s yearly rhythm.

Includes:

- operating year definition
- election timeline
- officer transition timeline
- contest scheduling
- annual events
- holiday exceptions

Operating constraints documented:

- club meets Mondays
- no meetings on federal holidays falling on Monday
- club closes final two weeks of December

---

## 11. Templates and Operational Tools

Standard templates used by the club.

Includes:

- meeting agenda template
- event planning template
- guest follow-up template
- officer transition checklist

These templates ensure consistency and reduce preparation time.

## 12. Club Website Operations

Documentation of the club website as a core public-facing operational asset.

Platform and domains:
- FreeToastHost
- tulsatoastmasters.org
- tulsatoastmasters.club

Includes:
- website purpose and role in guest acquisition
- content ownership and update responsibility
- required recurring content updates
- minimum information standards
- alignment with club events, meetings, and membership growth

Purpose:
Ensure the club website remains accurate, useful, and operationally integrated into the club’s guest and communication systems.

---

# Out of Scope

The following topics are outside the scope of this project.

---

## Toastmasters International Curriculum

The project will not reproduce:

- Pathways manuals
- full Toastmasters training material
- Toastmasters branding guides

The playbook references these where appropriate but does not replace them.

---

## District Governance

The playbook will not document:

- district officer responsibilities
- district conference procedures
- district-level governance systems

The playbook focuses strictly on **club-level operations**.

---

## Technology Platforms

The following are not included in this version:

- CRM systems
- membership databases
- website architecture
- email marketing systems

These may be addressed in future operational projects.

---

## Financial Planning Beyond Club Operations

The project will not include:

- multi-year financial projections
- external fundraising
- advanced accounting systems

Treasurer procedures will focus on **basic club financial management**.

# Assumptions

This scope assumes the following conditions:

- Tulsa Club operates on a Monday evening schedule
- the club values strong guest integration
- the club maintains recurring annual traditions
- the club wishes to preserve institutional knowledge
- officers will use the playbook as guidance rather than rigid policy

---

# Constraints

The project is constrained by the following factors:

- volunteer officer availability
- venue layout limitations
- time available for documentation work
- current club practices and traditions

---

# Acceptance Criteria

The project scope will be considered fulfilled when:

- every major operational domain is documented
- all SOPs contain actionable instructions
- the playbook aligns with actual Tulsa Club practices
- the documentation is usable by future officers
- the documentation supports annual continuity

---

# Review and Maintenance

The scope document should be reviewed:

- after the first full draft of the playbook
- during officer transition each year
- when major club operational changes occur

Future playbook revisions should update this scope if operational domains expand.
