# Purpose

The Annual Operating Calendar defines the predictable yearly rhythm of the Tulsa Club Toastmasters program.

This system ensures that the club maintains consistency in:

- leadership transition
- contests
- recruitment activities
- celebration events
- operational planning

By documenting the yearly structure, the club preserves institutional continuity and enables new officers to plan effectively.

---

# Operating Year

The Tulsa Club Toastmasters operating year follows the Toastmasters International calendar:

July 1 – June 30

This calendar governs:

- officer terms
- contest seasons
- leadership transitions
- planning cycles

---

# Weekly Meeting Rhythm

Standard meeting schedule:

| Item | Standard |
|-----|---------|
Meeting Day | Monday |
Doors Open | 6:15 PM |
Meeting Start | 6:30 PM |
Meeting End | 7:45 PM |
Post-Meeting Networking | 7:45–8:00 PM |

Meeting duration target:

75 minutes.

---

# Holiday Exceptions

The club does **not meet** when:

- a U.S. federal holiday falls on Monday
- the final two Mondays of December

This allows members time for travel and family commitments.

---

# Annual Event Calendar

The Tulsa Club maintains a predictable annual sequence of key events.

| Month | Event |
|------|------|
July | Leadership Showcase & Officer Induction |
September | Fall Speech Contest |
October | Club Anniversary Celebration |
December | Christmas Party (2nd Monday) |
January | International Speech Contest |
March | Open House / Membership Event |
June | Year-End Reflection / Transition Preparation |

These anchor events provide rhythm to the club year.

---

# Leadership Cycle

The club leadership cycle follows the Toastmasters governance calendar.

| Month | Activity |
|------|---------|
April | Begin officer nominations |
May | Officer elections |
June | Leadership transition preparation |
July | Officer induction and new year launch |

This ensures leadership continuity.

---

# Contest Cycle

Two contest cycles occur each year.

## Fall Contest Cycle

Typical month:

September

Contest type:

- Humorous Speech **or**
- Tall Tales

Secondary contest:

- Evaluation **or**
- Table Topics

Purpose:

Engagement and early-year participation.

---

## Winter Contest Cycle

Typical month:

January

Contest type:

- International Speech Contest

Secondary contest:

- Evaluation **or**
- Table Topics

Purpose:

Identify speakers who may advance to higher contest levels.

---

# Membership Growth Cycle

Membership growth is supported through periodic recruitment efforts.

Typical rhythm:

| Period | Focus |
|------|------|
Late Summer | guest invitations |
Fall | member engagement |
Winter | guest follow-ups |
Spring | Open House recruitment |

The Open House event is the primary structured recruitment meeting.

---

# Annual Planning Timeline

The officer team should review the calendar annually.

Recommended review:

**June officer transition meeting**

Topics to confirm:

- event dates
- contest timing
- recruitment activities
- meeting themes

Adjustments may be made based on club needs.

# Monthly Operational Rhythm

Each month typically includes:

- 3–4 regular meetings
- occasional special events
- guest integration
- speech development

The Vice President Education maintains the meeting schedule.
