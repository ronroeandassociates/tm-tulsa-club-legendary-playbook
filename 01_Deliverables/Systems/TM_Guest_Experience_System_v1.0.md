# Purpose

The Guest Experience System defines how Tulsa Club welcomes, engages, and converts visitors into members.

A consistent guest experience ensures that:

- visitors feel welcomed immediately
- guests understand the purpose of Toastmasters
- guests have an opportunity to participate
- guests are invited to return
- interested guests become members

This system supports sustainable club growth.

---

# Guest Journey

The guest experience follows a structured journey:

1. Awareness
2. Arrival
3. Orientation
4. Participation
5. Connection
6. Follow-Up
7. Membership Invitation

Each stage contributes to the overall guest experience.

---

# Stage 1 – Awareness

Guests typically discover the club through:

- the club website  
  - tulsatoastmasters.org  
  - tulsatoastmasters.club
- social media
- personal invitations from members
- Toastmasters International club directory
- community word of mouth
- special events or Open Houses

The website must clearly communicate:

- meeting time
- meeting location
- guest welcome message
- how to attend a meeting

---

# Stage 2 – Arrival

Guests should be greeted immediately when entering the meeting.

A **Guest Ambassador** should be designated at each meeting.

Responsibilities include:

- welcoming guests
- helping them sign in
- introducing them to members
- seating them with experienced members

No guest should remain unacknowledged.

---

# Stage 3 – Orientation

At the start of the meeting, guests should receive a brief explanation of the meeting format.

Key points to explain:

- Toastmasters helps improve communication and leadership skills
- meetings include prepared speeches, Table Topics, and evaluations
- guests are welcome to participate but are never required to speak

This reduces guest anxiety.

---

# Stage 4 – Participation

Guests should be encouraged (but not pressured) to participate.

Participation opportunities include:

- Table Topics
- introductions at their table
- networking discussions
- Speak & Connect activities during Open House events

Participation helps guests experience the value of the club.

---

# Stage 5 – Connection

Members should intentionally engage guests during the meeting.

Best practices include:

- inviting guests to join table conversations
- introducing guests to multiple members
- asking about guest interests and goals

Strong personal connection increases the likelihood that guests will return.

---

# Stage 6 – Follow-Up

Guest follow-up should occur within **24 hours of the meeting**.

The Vice President Membership or Guest Ambassador should send:

- a thank-you message
- encouragement to return
- information about joining the club
- the date of the next meeting

Prompt follow-up significantly improves membership conversion.

---

# Stage 7 – Membership Invitation

Guests should receive a clear invitation to join when appropriate.

This may occur:

- during Open House meetings
- during personal conversations
- through follow-up communication

The invitation should explain:

- membership benefits
- meeting schedule
- dues structure
- next steps to join

Membership forms should be available.

---

# Guest Sign-In Process

Guests should sign in when they arrive.

Information collected:

| Field | Required |
|------|---------|
Name | Yes |
Email | Yes |
Phone | Optional |
How they heard about the club | Optional |

The sign-in sheet enables follow-up communication.

---

# Guest Seating

Guests should be seated with experienced members.

Because the venue uses **tables with four seats**, the best arrangement is:

- one guest per table when possible
- two experienced members at each table
- one newer member if available

This ensures conversation and support.

---

# Guest Ambassadors

Each meeting should designate at least one **Guest Ambassador**.

Typical responsibilities include:

- greeting guests
- assisting with sign-in
- introducing guests to members
- explaining meeting format
- checking on guests after the meeting

This role significantly improves guest comfort.

---

# Website Integration

The club website supports the guest pipeline.

Domains:

- tulsatoastmasters.org
- tulsatoastmasters.club

The website should always provide:

- meeting time and location
- clear invitation for guests
- description of Toastmasters
- contact information

Website accuracy should be reviewed regularly.

---

# Open House Integration

Open House events are designed to attract multiple guests.

The Guest Experience System is used during Open House meetings with additional emphasis on:

- structured networking
- interactive speaking activities
- clear membership invitations

---

# Conversion Metrics

Healthy clubs typically experience:

| Metric | Target |
|------|-------|
Guests per meeting | 1–3 |
Guests per Open House | 10+ |
Guest return rate | 30–50% |
Membership conversion | 20–40% |

Tracking these metrics helps evaluate club growth.

---

# Common Failure Points

| Issue | Prevention |
|------|-------------|
Guests ignored | assign guest ambassadors |
Guests confused | provide orientation |
No follow-up | VP Membership follow-up within 24 hours |
Guests sit together | distribute guests among tables |

---

# Success Indicators

The guest system is successful when:

- guests feel welcomed immediately
- guests participate comfortably
- guests return for additional meetings
- membership grows steadily
