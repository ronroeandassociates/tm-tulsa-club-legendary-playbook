# Purpose

The Room Layout Standard defines how the Tulsa Club meeting space is organized for weekly meetings and special events.

The goal of this system is to ensure that the meeting environment supports:

- clear speaker visibility
- easy guest integration
- efficient hospitality setup
- minimal room rearrangement
- strong audience engagement

Because the venue uses fixed table seating, the club uses a **table-oriented layout model** rather than a traditional chair-only arrangement.

---

# Venue Characteristics

The Tulsa Club meeting venue has the following characteristics:

- square tables
- four chairs per table
- consistent table placement
- limited need or desire to rearrange furniture each week

The club uses a **center speaking area model** that works with this configuration.

---

# Standard Weekly Layout

The room should maintain a consistent layout for weekly meetings.

Key areas include:

- speaking area
- audience tables
- guest sign-in
- hospitality table
- banner placement

Members rotate their chairs toward the speaker when necessary.

---

# Basic Layout Model

Typical weekly layout:


       Toastmasters Banner

          Speaker Area

 Table 1           Table 2

 Table 3           Table 4


Guest Sign-In       Hospitality Table



This arrangement ensures that all participants have a clear view of the speaker.

---

# Speaking Area

The speaking area should remain clear of obstacles.

Guidelines:

- allow approximately 8–10 feet of open space
- ensure clear visibility from all tables
- place the Toastmasters banner behind or beside the speaker

The speaking area should remain consistent each week.

---

# Audience Tables

Audience seating is organized by tables.

Best practices:

- encourage members to rotate chairs toward the speaker
- distribute guests across tables
- avoid large empty gaps between tables

Tables should support both:

- speech observation
- small group interaction

---

# Guest Sign-In Area

The guest sign-in table should be located near the entrance.

Responsibilities include:

- collecting guest information
- greeting visitors
- distributing name tags if used

The Guest Ambassador should monitor this area before the meeting begins.

---

# Hospitality Placement

Hospitality tables should be placed:

- along the side wall
- or at the rear of the room

Guidelines:

- do not block the speaking area
- allow easy access before and after meetings
- maintain clean surfaces and clear trash areas

Hospitality should support networking rather than distract from the meeting.

---

# Banner Placement

The Toastmasters banner should be placed:

- behind the speaker
- or slightly to the side of the speaking area

The banner serves several purposes:

- visual focus for the room
- professional appearance
- backdrop for photos
- reinforcement of Toastmasters branding

---

# Pre-Meeting Setup Procedure

The Sergeant at Arms or setup volunteers should prepare the room approximately **15–20 minutes before the meeting**.

Typical tasks include:

- confirm speaking area is clear
- position the Toastmasters banner
- place guest sign-in materials
- prepare hospitality tables if applicable
- ensure chairs face toward the speaking area

---

# Special Event Adjustments

Certain events may require minor adjustments.

## Open House

- expand networking space
- emphasize guest sign-in area
- allow extra space near hospitality tables

---

## Speech Contests

- create clear contestant speaking space
- ensure judges have unobstructed view
- maintain audience seating stability

---

## Anniversary or Christmas Events

- allow more conversational space
- increase hospitality area
- reduce emphasis on formal speaking layout

# Common Failure Points

| Issue | Prevention |
|------|-------------|
Speaker blocked by tables | maintain clear speaking area |
Guests unsure where to go | place sign-in near entrance |
Food distracts from meeting | keep hospitality tables away from speaker |
Members face away from speaker | encourage chair rotation |

---

# Success Indicators

The room layout system is successful when:

- all attendees can see the speaker clearly
- guests are welcomed immediately upon arrival
- the meeting environment feels organized and professional
- minimal setup effort is required each week

---

# Review and Update

This system should be reviewed annually during:

- officer transition
- venue changes
- meeting format adjustments

Updates should maintain compatibility with the venue layout.
