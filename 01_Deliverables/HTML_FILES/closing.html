Meeting Role Script

Meeting Closing

The meeting closing reinforces the value of the meeting, thanks participants, and ensures guests know what to do next.

Role Purpose

The closing creates a strong final impression. It should be brief, positive, and leave members and guests feeling energized to return.

Primary Responsibilities

  • Thank speakers, evaluators, and role holders
  • Recognize guests and invite them back
  • Highlight upcoming meetings or events
  • Formally adjourn the meeting

What Good Looks Like

  • The closing is brief and confident
  • Members feel appreciated
  • Guests clearly understand how to return
  • The meeting ends on a positive note

Sample Script

Closing Example:

"Thank you to everyone who contributed to tonight's meeting — our speakers, evaluators, and all role holders who made the meeting possible."

"To our guests, we are glad you joined us this evening. Toastmasters is a place where communication and leadership skills grow through practice and encouragement."

"We meet every Monday, and we would love to see you again. If you enjoyed tonight's meeting, please come back and consider becoming a member."

"This concludes tonight's meeting of Tulsa Toastmasters Club #148. The meeting is now adjourned."

Quick Tips

End with Energy

Your tone should leave the room feeling positive and complete.

Recognize Effort

Thank the people who made the meeting work.

Invite Guests Back

Guests should leave knowing exactly how to return.
