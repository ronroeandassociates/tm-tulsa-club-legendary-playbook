Meeting Role Script

Presiding Officer

The Presiding Officer opens the meeting, establishes tone, welcomes members and guests, and transfers the meeting smoothly to the Toastmaster or next leader in the agenda.

Jump to Sample Script Meeting-Day Checklist

Role Purpose

Your role is to create a strong start. The opening should feel confident, warm, organized, and on mission. You help the meeting begin with clarity and momentum.

What You Do

  • Call the meeting to order on time.
  • Welcome members, guests, and visitors.
  • Introduce the theme, word of the day, or important club announcements if appropriate.
  • Set expectations and hand the meeting off cleanly.

What Good Looks Like

  • The meeting starts on time.
  • Guests feel welcomed immediately.
  • The opening is polished but not long.
  • The transition to the Toastmaster is smooth and confident.

Before the Meeting

  1. Review the agenda and confirm who is handling each major segment.
  2. Know the meeting theme, word of the day, and any priority announcements.
  3. Identify guests, visitors, or special recognitions in advance if possible.
  4. Be prepared to begin promptly at the scheduled start time.
At Tulsa Club, the presiding officer is normally the Club President. The role should establish authority, warmth, and order without taking over the whole meeting.

Opening Priorities

Start Strong

  • Stand confidently
  • Call the room to order
  • Open on time

Welcome Clearly

  • Acknowledge members and guests
  • Create a hospitable tone
  • Make new people feel included

Frame the Meeting

  • Introduce the theme if used
  • Share a key announcement if needed
  • Move the meeting forward without delay

Sample Script

Adjust this to the meeting theme, guest count, and any major club announcements.

Opening:

“Good evening, everyone. The meeting of Tulsa Toastmasters Club #148 will now come to order.”

“Welcome, members, guests, and visitors. We are glad you are here. Toastmasters is a supportive environment where we build communication and leadership skills through practice, feedback, and encouragement.”

“Our theme for tonight is ‘The Confidence Code,’ and our word of the day is ‘Code’ — a set of principles or patterns that guide behavior.”

“If you are a guest with us tonight, welcome. We are honored to have you, and we hope you enjoy the meeting.”

“At this time, it is my privilege to introduce our Toastmaster for the evening, Ron Olsen.”

Meeting-Day Checklist

  • Arrive early and review the agenda.
  • Confirm the theme, word of the day, and announcements.
  • Identify guests and visitors if possible.
  • Start the meeting on time.
  • Keep the opening warm, concise, and confident.
  • Hand off to the Toastmaster cleanly.

Quick Tips

Set the Tone

Your opening defines the room. Speak with calm authority and energy.

Honor the Guests

Guests decide quickly whether they feel welcome. A strong greeting matters.

Do Not Drift

Keep the opening focused. Long openings reduce momentum before the meeting really begins.
