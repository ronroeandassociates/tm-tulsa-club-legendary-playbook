Meeting Role Script

Table Topics Master

The Table Topics Master leads the impromptu speaking portion of the meeting, helping members practice thinking and speaking on their feet.

Role Purpose

Table Topics gives members the opportunity to practice impromptu speaking. The goal is to encourage participation, creativity, and confidence.

Primary Responsibilities

  • Prepare several engaging questions or prompts
  • Call on participants who do not already have a speaking role
  • Keep the session energetic and moving
  • Support speakers with clear introductions

What Good Looks Like

  • Questions are clear and interesting
  • Many members have an opportunity to participate
  • The energy of the meeting increases
  • Transitions are smooth and confident

Preparation Tips

  • Prepare 6–10 questions in advance.
  • Use a theme if helpful (seasonal, conference, leadership, etc.).
  • Make prompts open-ended.
  • Avoid calling on members who already have speaking roles.
  • Encourage guests to participate if they are comfortable.

Sample Script

Introduction:

"Table Topics is the portion of our meeting where we practice impromptu speaking. Speakers will have 1 to 2 minutes to respond to a question."

"I will call a speaker first and then give them the question so that their response remains spontaneous."

Example Prompt:

"Andrew, your question is: If you could learn any new skill instantly, what would it be and why?"

"Our next speaker will be Wanda…"

Quick Tips

Call the Speaker First

This ensures the response remains spontaneous.

Keep Questions Clear

Complex prompts can confuse speakers.

Maintain Energy

Your enthusiasm sets the tone for the session.
