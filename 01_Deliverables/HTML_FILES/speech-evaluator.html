Speech Evaluator

The Speech Evaluator gives focused, constructive feedback that helps a speaker improve. A strong evaluation is specific, encouraging, and aligned with the speaker’s project objectives.

Jump to Sample Script Meeting-Day Checklist

Role Purpose

Your job is to listen carefully, observe the speech against its objectives, and deliver feedback that is honest, useful, and motivating. The evaluation should help the speaker know what worked and what to improve next.

What You Do

  • Review the speaker’s project objectives before the meeting if available.
  • Listen for content, structure, delivery, and audience connection.
  • Identify clear strengths and targeted growth areas.
  • Deliver a concise oral evaluation and complete any written evaluation as needed.

What Good Looks Like

  • Your feedback is specific, not vague.
  • You affirm what worked well.
  • You recommend one or two meaningful improvements.
  • Your tone builds the speaker rather than discouraging them.

Preparation Before the Meeting

  1. Review the speaker’s name, speech title, and project if available.
  2. Read the Pathways objectives or manual objectives before the meeting when possible.
  3. Plan to listen for organization, vocal variety, body language, clarity, and effectiveness.
  4. Bring a pen and note page or device so you can capture observations in real time.
Best practice: speakers should post their title, introduction, and project objectives in advance whenever possible. That gives evaluators time to prepare and makes the evaluation materially stronger.

Evaluation Framework

A simple structure keeps the evaluation clear and balanced.

1 · Open Strong

Start with Value

Identify what the speaker did well and why it worked. Begin with genuine strengths.

2 · Be Specific

Use Evidence

Point to concrete moments from the speech rather than giving generic praise or criticism.

3 · Recommend Growth

Offer Next Step

Give one or two improvements the speaker can actually apply in the next speech.

4 · End Encouragingly

Close with Confidence

Leave the speaker feeling supported, capable, and motivated to keep building.

What to Watch For

Content

  • Clear message
  • Relevant examples
  • Strong opening and conclusion

Organization

  • Logical flow
  • Easy-to-follow structure
  • Effective transitions

Delivery

  • Vocal variety
  • Eye contact
  • Body language and stage presence

Audience Impact

  • Connection
  • Clarity
  • Memorable moments

Sample Script

Adjust this to fit the speech, the project objectives, and the meeting tone.

Opening:

“Thank you, Madam Toastmaster. Today I had the privilege of evaluating Madeline’s speech, ‘Finding My Voice.’”

Strengths:

“Madeline, you connected well with the audience from the start. Your opening story immediately drew us in, and your vocal pace made the message easy to follow. I also appreciated your clear structure, which helped the audience stay with you throughout the speech.”

Improvement:

“One area to strengthen in your next speech would be pausing after key points. A slightly longer pause would give your strongest ideas more weight and allow the audience more time to absorb them.”

Close:

“Overall, this was a well-delivered speech with strong audience connection and a clear message. Thank you for sharing it with us, and I look forward to your next speech.”

Meeting-Day Checklist

  • Review the speech title and project objectives before the meeting if possible.
  • Bring notes or an evaluation form.
  • Listen for both strengths and growth opportunities.
  • Use examples from the speech to support your comments.
  • Keep the oral evaluation within the allotted time.
  • End with encouragement and clarity.

Quick Tips

Be Specific

“Great speech” is not enough. Tell the speaker what specifically made it effective.

Be Balanced

A good evaluation includes both affirmation and improvement, not one without the other.

Be Practical

Give suggestions the speaker can realistically apply next time.
