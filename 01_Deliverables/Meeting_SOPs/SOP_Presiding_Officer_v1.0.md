# Purpose

The Presiding Officer officially opens and closes the meeting.

This role establishes meeting order, introduces the Toastmaster of the Day, and ensures the meeting begins and ends professionally.

The Presiding Officer is typically the **Club President**, but another officer may serve if necessary.

---

# Core Responsibilities

The Presiding Officer is responsible for:

- calling the meeting to order
- welcoming members and guests
- introducing the Toastmaster of the Day
- making brief club announcements
- closing the meeting

This role sets the professional tone of the meeting.

---

# Preparation

Preparation should begin before the meeting.

Tasks include:

- reviewing the meeting agenda
- confirming the Toastmaster of the Day
- preparing announcements if necessary

Preparation ensures a smooth meeting opening.

---

# Arrival

The Presiding Officer should arrive **15–20 minutes before the meeting**.

Arrival tasks include:

- greeting members and guests
- coordinating with the Sergeant at Arms
- reviewing the agenda

Early arrival reinforces leadership presence.

---

# Opening the Meeting

Typical opening sequence:

1. Call meeting to order
2. Welcome members and guests
3. Introduce guests
4. Introduce the Toastmaster of the Day

Example:

“Good evening and welcome to the Tulsa Club Toastmasters meeting.”

---

# Guest Recognition

Guests should be acknowledged early in the meeting.

Approaches may include:

- asking guests to introduce themselves briefly
- welcoming them on behalf of the club

Guests should always feel appreciated for attending.

---

# Announcements

The Presiding Officer may make brief announcements such as:

- upcoming events
- contests
- club milestones

Announcements should remain concise.

---

# Introducing the Toastmaster

The Presiding Officer then transfers meeting control.

Example:

“It is now my pleasure to introduce our Toastmaster of the Day.”

The Toastmaster then manages the meeting program.

---

# Closing the Meeting

After evaluations conclude, the Presiding Officer closes the meeting.

Typical closing tasks:

- thank meeting participants
- recognize guests
- invite guests to return
- remind members of the next meeting

Example closing:

“This concludes tonight’s meeting of Tulsa Club Toastmasters.”

---

# Time Awareness

The Presiding Officer should:

- start the meeting on time
- close the meeting on time

Punctual meetings respect members’ schedules.

---

# Common Failure Points


| Issue                  | Prevention         |
| ---------------------- | ------------------ |
| Late start             | arrive early       |
| Too many announcements | keep remarks brief |
| Guests overlooked      | acknowledge early  |
| Closing delayed        | monitor schedule   |


---

# Success Indicators

The Presiding Officer is successful when:

- meetings begin promptly
- guests feel welcomed
- transitions to the Toastmaster are smooth
- meetings conclude professionally
