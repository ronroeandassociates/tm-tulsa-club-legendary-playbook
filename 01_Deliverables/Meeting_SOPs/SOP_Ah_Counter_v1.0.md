# Purpose

The Ah-Counter helps members improve their speaking clarity by identifying verbal fillers and unnecessary sounds used during speeches.

Common filler words include:

- “ah”
- “um”
- “uh”
- “you know”
- “like”
- repeated phrases

The goal of the Ah-Counter role is to increase speaker awareness and encourage more polished speaking habits.

---

# Core Responsibilities

The Ah-Counter is responsible for:

- listening for filler words during the meeting
- recording the number of verbal fillers used by each speaker
- reporting these observations during the evaluation segment

This role helps speakers recognize and reduce distracting speech habits.

---

# Preparation

Preparation requirements are minimal.

Before the meeting the Ah-Counter should:

- review the meeting agenda
- prepare a simple note sheet
- understand which speakers will be observed

This ensures accurate tracking during the meeting.

---

# Arrival

The Ah-Counter should arrive **15–20 minutes before the meeting begins**.

Arrival tasks include:

- reviewing the agenda
- confirming the speaking order
- coordinating with the General Evaluator

Early preparation helps ensure accurate observation.

---

# Filler Words to Observe

The Ah-Counter should listen for common verbal fillers such as:

| Filler Type | Examples |
|-------------|----------|
Verbal fillers | ah, um, uh |
Repeated phrases | you know, like |
Pause fillers | well, so |
Other sounds | er, hmm |

Not every pause or filler should be counted excessively. The goal is awareness, not criticism.

---

# Tracking Speakers

The Ah-Counter typically observes:

- Table Topics speakers
- prepared speech speakers
- evaluators

Tracking should remain simple and accurate.

Example tracking format:

| Speaker | Filler Count |
|--------|-------------|
Jane | 3 |
Mark | 2 |
Sarah | 4 |

---

# Observation Guidelines

The Ah-Counter should:

- listen carefully
- track fillers consistently
- remain objective
- avoid interrupting speakers

Observations should remain supportive and constructive.

---

# Ah-Counter Report

During the evaluation segment, the Ah-Counter presents a brief report.

The report should include:

- each speaker’s filler word count
- general observations about filler usage

Example:

“Jane used three filler words during her speech.”

The report should remain concise.

---

# Encouragement Approach

The Ah-Counter’s report should remain encouraging.

Purpose of the report:

- increase awareness
- help speakers improve
- support constructive feedback

The role is educational, not critical.

---

# Integration with Evaluation Team

The Ah-Counter works as part of the evaluation team alongside:

- General Evaluator
- Timer
- Grammarian

Together these roles provide a comprehensive evaluation of speaking performance.

---

# Common Failure Points

| Issue | Prevention |
|------|-------------|
Tracking too many sounds | focus on clear filler patterns |
Missing speakers | follow the agenda carefully |
Report too long | keep results concise |
Overly critical tone | remain supportive |

---

# Success Indicators

The Ah-Counter role is successful when:

- speakers become aware of filler habits
- filler usage gradually decreases
- the report helps improve speaking clarity

---

# Continuous Improvement

Members serving as Ah-Counter should:

- listen carefully for filler patterns
- maintain accurate records
- provide clear and concise reports

Awareness of filler words helps members become more confident and polished speakers.
