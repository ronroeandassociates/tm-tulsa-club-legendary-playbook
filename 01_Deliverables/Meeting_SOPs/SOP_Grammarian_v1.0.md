# Purpose

The Grammarian supports language development during the meeting by observing and reporting on word usage.

This role encourages members to:

- expand vocabulary
- improve grammar
- develop more effective speaking habits
- become more conscious of language choices

The Grammarian contributes to the educational value of each meeting.

---

# Core Responsibilities

The Grammarian is responsible for:

- introducing the Word of the Day
- listening for effective or interesting language usage
- noting grammar improvements where appropriate
- presenting a brief language report during the evaluation segment

The Grammarian focuses on encouraging good language habits.

---

# Preparation

Preparation should begin before the meeting.

Preparation tasks include:

- selecting a **Word of the Day**
- preparing a clear definition
- choosing a word that is practical and easy to use

The Word of the Day should be relevant and accessible to speakers.

---

# Selecting the Word of the Day

The Word of the Day should meet the following criteria:

- useful in everyday speech
- easy to pronounce
- adaptable to different speaking topics

Example:

| Word | Definition |
|------|-----------|
Clarity | The quality of being clear and easy to understand |

The Grammarian should encourage participants to incorporate the word into their speeches.

---

# Arrival

The Grammarian should arrive **15–20 minutes before the meeting**.

Arrival tasks include:

- confirming the meeting agenda
- writing the Word of the Day where visible if possible
- coordinating with the General Evaluator

Preparation ensures the role runs smoothly.

---

# Introducing the Word of the Day

When introduced by the Toastmaster, the Grammarian should:

- clearly state the Word of the Day
- provide the definition
- use the word in a sample sentence
- encourage members to use the word during the meeting

Example introduction:

“Tonight’s Word of the Day is *clarity*, which means the quality of being clear and easy to understand.”

---

# Observing Language Usage

Throughout the meeting the Grammarian listens for:

- effective vocabulary
- descriptive language
- interesting phrases
- strong word choices

The Grammarian may also note minor grammar improvements if helpful.

Observations should remain supportive rather than critical.

---

# Language Notes

The Grammarian should keep brief notes during the meeting.

Typical observations include:

- examples of strong word usage
- effective phrases
- successful use of the Word of the Day

These notes support the final report.

---

# Grammarian Report

During the evaluation segment the Grammarian presents a brief language report.

The report may include:

- successful uses of the Word of the Day
- examples of strong language usage
- suggestions for improvement if appropriate

The report should be concise and encouraging.

---

# Integration with Meeting Roles

The Grammarian works within the evaluation team and coordinates with:

- General Evaluator
- Ah-Counter
- Timer

These roles together provide feedback on communication performance.

---

# Educational Value

The Grammarian helps members develop:

- stronger vocabulary
- clearer communication
- greater language awareness

These skills benefit both prepared and impromptu speaking.

---

# Common Failure Points

| Issue | Prevention |
|------|-------------|
Word of the Day too difficult | choose practical vocabulary |
Overly critical feedback | focus on encouragement |
Report too long | keep observations brief |
Forgetting examples | take notes during meeting |

---

# Success Indicators

The Grammarian role is successful when:

- members attempt to use the Word of the Day
- speakers demonstrate thoughtful language choices
- the language report provides useful insights

---

# Continuous Improvement

Members serving as Grammarian should:

- select engaging vocabulary
- observe strong speakers
- refine their language observations

Improving language awareness helps members become more effective communicators.
