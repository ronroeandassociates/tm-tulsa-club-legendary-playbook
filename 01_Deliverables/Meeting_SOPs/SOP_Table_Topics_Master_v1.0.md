# Purpose

The Table Topics Master leads the impromptu speaking segment of the meeting.

This segment helps members practice:

- thinking quickly
- organizing thoughts
- speaking confidently without preparation

Table Topics is often one of the most energetic parts of the meeting and helps guests experience Toastmasters participation.

---

# Core Responsibilities

The Table Topics Master is responsible for:

- preparing impromptu speaking questions
- explaining the purpose of Table Topics
- selecting participants
- maintaining an engaging pace
- encouraging participation from members and guests

This role helps develop confidence in spontaneous speaking.

---

# Preparation

Preparation should begin several days before the meeting.

Preparation tasks include:

- developing 6–8 questions or prompts
- selecting questions appropriate for the meeting theme (if used)
- ensuring questions are simple and engaging
- avoiding controversial or sensitive topics

Prepared questions create a smoother and more enjoyable segment.

---

# Arrival

The Table Topics Master should arrive **15–20 minutes before the meeting**.

Arrival tasks include:

- reviewing the meeting agenda
- confirming the Timer is prepared
- coordinating with the Toastmaster of the Day

Early preparation ensures the segment begins smoothly.

---

# Explaining Table Topics

When introduced by the Toastmaster, the Table Topics Master should briefly explain the segment.

Example explanation:

“Table Topics provides an opportunity to practice impromptu speaking. Participants are invited to respond to a question or prompt with a short speech.”

Guests should understand that participation is voluntary.

---

# Question Design

Effective Table Topics questions should be:

- easy to understand
- open-ended
- conversational
- relevant to everyday experiences

Examples:

- What is a skill you would like to improve this year?
- What advice would you give your younger self?
- What is a memorable travel experience you’ve had?

Questions should encourage storytelling and reflection.

---

# Participant Selection

Participants should be selected in a way that encourages broad participation.

Best practices include:

- calling on members who do not already have meeting roles
- inviting guests if they feel comfortable
- distributing opportunities across different tables

This approach supports the **Table Participation System**.

---

# Table-Based Selection

Because Tulsa Club uses table seating, the Table Topics Master may occasionally select speakers by table.

Example approach:

“Let’s hear from someone at Table 2.”

The table can quickly choose a volunteer.

This approach:

- reduces hesitation
- encourages teamwork
- increases participation.

---

# Guest Participation

Guests may be invited to participate but should never feel pressured.

A gentle invitation might include:

“Guests are welcome to participate if they feel comfortable.”

Guest participation often creates a memorable first experience.

---

# Timing

Standard Table Topics speaking time:

| Signal | Time |
|------|------|
Green | 1 minute |
Yellow | 1 minute 30 seconds |
Red | 2 minutes |

Participants should aim to speak within the timing guidelines.

---

# Segment Length

The Table Topics segment typically lasts:

10–15 minutes.

The Table Topics Master should manage the number of speakers accordingly.

Typical number of participants:

5–7 speakers.

---

# Energy Management

The Table Topics Master should maintain strong meeting energy by:

- speaking clearly and confidently
- encouraging applause
- keeping transitions brief
- selecting a variety of speakers

A lively Table Topics session energizes the meeting.

---

# Integration with Systems

This role supports the following meeting systems:

- TM_Table_Participation_System_v1.0.md
- TM_Guest_Experience_System_v1.0.md

Table Topics often provides the first speaking opportunity for guests and new members.

---

# Common Failure Points

| Issue | Prevention |
|------|-------------|
Questions too complicated | keep prompts simple |
Calling only experienced members | include newer members |
Segment too long | limit number of speakers |
Low participation | invite speakers by table |

---

# Success Indicators

A successful Table Topics session includes:

- energetic participation
- supportive audience response
- guests feeling welcomed
- speakers practicing impromptu communication

---

# Continuous Improvement

Members serving as Table Topics Master should:

- experiment with creative questions
- observe effective Table Topics leaders
- refine pacing and audience engagement

Improving this role strengthens meeting participation and energy.
