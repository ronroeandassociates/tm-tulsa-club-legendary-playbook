# Purpose

The Prepared Speaker delivers a structured speech designed to improve communication skills.

Prepared speeches allow members to practice:

- speech organization
- storytelling
- persuasive communication
- confidence in public speaking

Prepared speeches are one of the most important learning components of the Toastmasters meeting.

---

# Core Responsibilities

The Prepared Speaker is responsible for:

- preparing a structured speech
- practicing the speech before the meeting
- delivering the speech within the assigned time
- listening carefully to the evaluator’s feedback
- applying feedback to future speeches

Each speech contributes to the speaker’s development.

---

# Preparation

Preparation should begin several days before the meeting.

Preparation tasks include:

- selecting a topic
- organizing the speech structure
- practicing delivery
- confirming the speech title
- confirming timing requirements

Practice improves confidence and delivery quality.

---

# Speech Structure

Effective speeches typically include three parts:

| Section | Purpose |
|-------|--------|
Opening | capture attention |
Body | deliver the message |
Conclusion | reinforce the key idea |

A clear structure helps the audience follow the message.

---

# Confirming Speech Details

Before the meeting the speaker should confirm:

- speech title
- speech project or objective
- assigned evaluator
- speech time range

This information helps the Toastmaster introduce the speaker properly.

---

## Submitting Speech Information to the Website

Speakers should submit their speech details through the club website as soon as possible.

Preferred timeline:

At least **24 hours before the meeting**.

Speaker roles are typically assigned **3 weeks in advance**, so submitting information early helps the Toastmaster and evaluator prepare.

The speaker should log into the club website and enter the following information:

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
Path | Toastmasters Pathways track |
Level | Level of the Pathways project |
Project Topic | Name of the project |
Speech Title | Title of the speech |
Speaker Introduction | Brief introduction read by the Toastmaster |

This information appears:

- on the **club website meeting agenda**
- on the **printed agenda (back page)**

Providing this information early ensures the meeting runs smoothly.

---

## Speaker Introduction

Speakers should provide a short introduction to assist the Toastmaster.

Typical introduction content:

- speaker name
- Pathways project
- brief background or context
- speech title

Example:

"John Smith is presenting Level 2 of the Presentation Mastery path. His speech tonight is titled *Finding Confidence Through Challenge.*"

The introduction should be **2–3 sentences** and provided on the website when possible.

---

## Benefits of Early Submission

Providing speech details early allows:

- the Toastmaster to prepare introductions
- evaluators to review project objectives
- the agenda to be accurate
- the meeting to run smoothly

---

# Arrival

Speakers should arrive **15–20 minutes before the meeting begins**.

Arrival tasks include:

- confirming attendance with the Toastmaster
- reviewing the introduction
- testing any visual aids if used

Early arrival reduces stress before speaking.

---

# During the Speech

When delivering the speech, the speaker should:

- maintain eye contact
- speak clearly and confidently
- stay within the assigned time
- focus on the key message

The goal is communication, not perfection.

---

# Timing Awareness

Speakers should watch for Timer signals.

Typical signals:

| Signal | Meaning |
|------|--------|
Green | minimum time reached |
Yellow | approaching limit |
Red | maximum time reached |

Speakers should aim to conclude near the target time.

---

# Audience Engagement

Strong speakers engage the audience by:

- using storytelling
- asking rhetorical questions
- varying vocal tone
- using gestures naturally

Audience engagement improves speech impact.

---

# After the Speech

After delivering the speech, the speaker should:

- listen carefully to the evaluator
- take notes on feedback
- reflect on strengths and improvement areas

Feedback is essential for growth.

---

# Integration with Meeting Roles

Prepared speakers work closely with:

- Toastmaster of the Day
- Speech Evaluator
- Timer

Clear coordination improves the speaking experience.

---

# Common Failure Points

| Issue | Prevention |
|------|-------------|
Insufficient preparation | practice several times |
Speech too long | rehearse timing |
Unclear message | organize speech structure |
Reading notes excessively | practice natural delivery |

---

# Success Indicators

A successful speech includes:

- clear message
- confident delivery
- audience engagement
- completion within time limits

---

# Continuous Improvement

Speakers should:

- seek feedback regularly
- observe other speakers
- practice storytelling and organization

Each speech builds confidence and skill.
