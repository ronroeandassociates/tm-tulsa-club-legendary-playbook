# Purpose

The Speech Evaluator provides constructive feedback to help the speaker improve.

Evaluations are a core learning element of Toastmasters because they help speakers:

- recognize strengths
- identify improvement opportunities
- develop communication skills

Effective evaluations are supportive, specific, and actionable.

---

# Core Responsibilities

The Speech Evaluator is responsible for:

- listening carefully to the speech
- evaluating speech strengths
- offering improvement suggestions
- delivering a structured evaluation report

The evaluator supports the speaker’s development.

---

# Preparation

Preparation begins before the meeting.

Preparation tasks include:

- reviewing the meeting agenda
- confirming the assigned speaker
- understanding the speech project objectives

Evaluators should understand what the speaker is trying to accomplish.

---

## Reviewing Speaker Information

Evaluators should review their assigned speaker’s information before the meeting.

This information can be found on the **club website meeting agenda**.

Evaluators should review:

| Item | Purpose |
|------|--------|
Path | Understand the speaker's development track |
Level | Identify experience stage |
Project | Understand speech objectives |
Speech Title | Understand topic context |
Speaker Introduction | Background information |

Evaluators should also review the **evaluation resources for the project** if available.

This preparation allows the evaluator to provide feedback aligned with the speaker’s learning objectives.

---

## Evaluation Preparation Timeline

Evaluators should review speaker information:

Preferably **24 hours before the meeting**.

Since speaking roles are assigned several weeks in advance, evaluators have time to prepare thoughtful feedback.

Prepared evaluators deliver stronger and more helpful evaluations.

---

# Evaluation Focus Areas

Evaluations typically consider several areas:

| Area | Observation |
|------|-------------|
Speech structure | clarity and organization |
Delivery | voice, gestures, eye contact |
Content | message effectiveness |
Audience engagement | connection with listeners |
Timing | staying within limits |

Observations should remain balanced.

---

# Listening During the Speech

While the speech is being delivered, the evaluator should:

- take brief notes
- identify strong moments
- identify opportunities for improvement

Evaluators should focus on helping the speaker grow.

---

# Evaluation Structure

A strong evaluation usually follows this pattern:

1. **Opening praise**
2. **Specific strengths**
3. **Constructive suggestions**
4. **Encouraging conclusion**

This structure keeps the evaluation balanced.

---

# Delivering the Evaluation

When presenting the evaluation, the evaluator should:

- speak directly to the speaker
- maintain a supportive tone
- be specific with feedback
- stay within the time limit

Evaluations should be helpful and encouraging.

---

# Timing

Typical evaluation timing:

| Signal | Time |
|------|------|
Green | 2 minutes |
Yellow | 2 minutes 30 seconds |
Red | 3 minutes |

Evaluations should remain concise.

---

# Constructive Feedback Principles

Effective evaluations follow these principles:

- focus on improvement
- recognize strengths
- provide clear suggestions
- remain respectful

The goal is to support speaker growth.

---

# Integration with Evaluation Team

The Speech Evaluator works with the evaluation team:

- General Evaluator
- Timer
- Grammarian
- Ah-Counter

These roles together provide complete feedback.

---

# Common Failure Points

| Issue | Prevention |
|------|-------------|
Too critical | balance praise and suggestions |
Too vague | give specific examples |
Evaluation too long | respect timing signals |
Ignoring speaker objectives | review project goals beforehand |

---

# Success Indicators

A successful evaluation:

- helps the speaker improve
- identifies specific strengths
- provides clear suggestions
- maintains a supportive tone

---

# Continuous Improvement

Evaluators should:

- observe experienced evaluators
- practice structured feedback
- refine listening and note-taking skills

Strong evaluators strengthen the entire club.
