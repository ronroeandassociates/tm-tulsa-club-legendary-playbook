# Purpose

The General Evaluator oversees the **evaluation segment of the meeting** and provides feedback on overall meeting performance.

This role ensures that:

- speakers receive useful evaluations
- meeting roles are reviewed
- meeting quality continually improves

The General Evaluator leads the evaluation team and manages the final segment of the meeting.

---

# Core Responsibilities

The General Evaluator is responsible for:

- coordinating speech evaluators
- managing the evaluation portion of the meeting
- calling on role reports (Timer, Grammarian, Ah-Counter)
- providing overall meeting evaluation
- offering constructive suggestions for improvement

This role helps maintain the educational quality of the club.

---

# Preparation

Preparation should begin several days before the meeting.

Tasks include:

- reviewing the meeting agenda
- confirming assigned evaluators
- understanding each speech project if possible
- preparing notes for meeting observation

Preparation helps the General Evaluator provide thoughtful feedback.

---

# Arrival

The General Evaluator should arrive **15–20 minutes before the meeting begins**.

Arrival tasks:

- confirm evaluators are present
- review evaluation assignments
- coordinate with the Toastmaster of the Day
- verify the Timer, Grammarian, and Ah-Counter understand their roles

Early preparation ensures a smooth evaluation segment.

---

# Evaluation Team

The evaluation team typically includes:

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
Speech Evaluators | evaluate prepared speeches |
Timer | track speaking time |
Grammarian | monitor word usage |
Ah-Counter | track filler words |

The General Evaluator coordinates this team.

---

# During the Meeting

Throughout the meeting, the General Evaluator should observe:

- meeting organization
- speaking effectiveness
- role performance
- audience engagement
- timing and pacing

Notes should be taken for the final evaluation.

---

# Evaluation Segment Introduction

When introduced by the Toastmaster, the General Evaluator should:

- briefly explain the purpose of evaluations
- introduce the evaluation team
- begin the evaluation sequence

Example:

“Evaluations are the heart of Toastmasters. They help speakers improve through supportive and constructive feedback.”

---

# Speech Evaluations

The General Evaluator calls on each speech evaluator.

Each evaluator should:

- give specific positive feedback
- offer suggestions for improvement
- respect time limits

The General Evaluator should thank each evaluator after their report.

---

# Role Reports

After speech evaluations, the General Evaluator calls on the meeting roles.

Typical order:

| Role | Report |
|------|-------|
Timer | speaking time report |
Grammarian | language observations |
Ah-Counter | filler word report |

Each report should remain brief.

---

# Overall Meeting Evaluation

The General Evaluator concludes the evaluation segment with an overall meeting assessment.

Topics may include:

- meeting organization
- speaking environment
- guest integration
- meeting timing
- audience engagement

Feedback should remain supportive and constructive.

---

# Integration with Systems

The General Evaluator should observe how the meeting systems are functioning, including:

- TM_Table_Participation_System_v1.0.md
- TM_Guest_Experience_System_v1.0.md
- TM_Room_Layout_Standard_v1.0.md

Observations may help improve these systems over time.

---

# Constructive Feedback Guidelines

Effective evaluations should follow these principles:

- be specific
- focus on improvement
- recognize strengths
- remain encouraging

The goal is improvement, not criticism.

---

# Time Management

The evaluation segment typically lasts:

15–20 minutes.

The General Evaluator should:

- ensure evaluators stay within time limits
- keep role reports concise
- deliver a brief overall evaluation

Maintaining schedule supports the meeting structure.

---

# Closing the Segment

At the end of the evaluation segment the General Evaluator returns control to the Toastmaster or President.

Example closing:

“This concludes the evaluation portion of our meeting.”

---

# Common Failure Points

| Issue | Prevention |
|------|-------------|
Evaluations too long | remind evaluators of time limits |
Evaluations too vague | encourage specific feedback |
Role reports disorganized | introduce speakers clearly |
Negative tone | keep feedback constructive |

---

# Success Indicators

The General Evaluator is successful when:

- speakers receive helpful feedback
- meeting roles are reviewed effectively
- the meeting concludes with constructive insights
- members feel encouraged to improve

---

# Continuous Improvement

Members serving as General Evaluator should:

- observe experienced evaluators
- practice structured feedback
- refine their meeting observations

Strong evaluators strengthen the entire club.
