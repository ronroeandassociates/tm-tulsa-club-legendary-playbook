# Purpose

The Treasurer manages the financial operations of the club.

This role ensures that club funds are handled responsibly and that required payments are made to Toastmasters International.

The Treasurer supports the club’s financial stability and transparency.

---

# Core Responsibilities

The Treasurer is responsible for:

- collecting membership dues
- maintaining the club bank account
- paying Toastmasters International membership fees
- tracking club income and expenses
- providing financial reports to the officer team

Clear financial management supports club operations.

---

# Membership Dues

Toastmasters membership dues are typically collected twice per year.

Standard collection periods include:

| Period | Payment Deadline |
|------|------------------|
April–September | March 31 |
October–March | September 30 |

Members should be reminded of dues deadlines in advance.

---

# Dues Collection

The Treasurer coordinates with the Vice President Membership to collect dues.

Collection methods may include:

- online payments
- checks
- cash payments

Payments should be recorded promptly.

---

# Toastmasters International Payments

The Treasurer is responsible for submitting membership payments to Toastmasters International.

This includes:

- new member fees
- semiannual membership dues

Payments should be submitted before official deadlines.

---

# Financial Recordkeeping

The Treasurer maintains records of:

| Record | Purpose |
|------|--------|
Membership payments | track dues collection |
Club expenses | track spending |
Bank account balance | financial oversight |
Payment confirmations | recordkeeping |

Accurate records support transparency.

---

# Financial Reporting

The Treasurer provides periodic financial updates to the officer team.

Typical reporting includes:

- current account balance
- dues collected
- outstanding payments
- recent expenses

Regular updates help the club monitor financial health.

---

# Expense Management

Typical club expenses may include:

- hospitality supplies
- event materials
- promotional materials
- venue-related costs

Expenses should be approved according to club policies.

---

# Budget Awareness

The Treasurer helps ensure the club maintains a sustainable financial position.

Best practices include:

- monitoring expenses
- maintaining reserve funds
- coordinating event spending with officers

Financial awareness supports long-term stability.

---

# Integration with Club Systems

- TM_Hospitality_System_v1.0.md

These systems may involve financial planning or spending.

|--------|---------------|

---

# Common Failure Points

| Issue | Prevention |
|------|-------------|
Late dues collection | send reminders early |
Missing payment records | record transactions immediately |
Overspending | review expenses with officers |
Missed TI payment deadlines | track payment calendar |

---

# Success Indicators

The Treasurer is successful when:

- dues are collected on time
- payments to Toastmasters International are submitted promptly
- financial records are accurate
- the club maintains a stable financial position

---

# Officer Transition

At the end of the term the Treasurer should:

- organize financial records
- transfer bank account access
- review financial procedures with the incoming Treasurer

Smooth transitions help maintain financial continuity.
