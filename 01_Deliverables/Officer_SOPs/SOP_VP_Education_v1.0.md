# Purpose

The Vice President Education (VPE) manages the educational program of the club.

The VPE ensures that meetings are well organized, members receive speaking opportunities, and the Toastmasters education program is delivered effectively.

This role coordinates the meeting agenda and supports member development.

---

# Core Responsibilities

The Vice President Education is responsible for:

- scheduling speakers and meeting roles
- maintaining the meeting agenda
- supporting member Pathways progress
- coordinating speech evaluators
- managing meeting themes when used
- ensuring meetings follow the club program structure

The VPE plays a critical role in maintaining meeting quality.

---

# Meeting Agenda Management

The VPE manages the **meeting agenda system** using the club website.

Responsibilities include:

- assigning meeting roles
- scheduling speakers
- confirming evaluators
- maintaining the agenda on the club website

Speaker roles are typically scheduled **at least three weeks in advance**.

This provides members sufficient time to prepare speeches.

---

# Website Agenda Workflow

The agenda is managed through the club website.

Members serving as speakers should submit the following information:

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
Path | Toastmasters Pathways track |
Level | Project level |
Project | Name of the project |
Speech Title | Title of the speech |
Speaker Introduction | Introduction for the Toastmaster |

Speakers should submit this information **preferably 24 hours before the meeting**.

This information appears:

- on the **club website meeting agenda**
- on the **printed agenda back page**

---

# Speaker Scheduling

The VPE ensures that members have regular speaking opportunities.

Best practices include:

- maintaining a balanced speaking schedule
- encouraging new members to speak early
- ensuring experienced members continue progressing

Speakers should not wait excessive time between speeches.

---

# Evaluator Assignments

Each speaker must have an assigned evaluator.

Evaluator responsibilities include:

- reviewing the speaker’s project
- preparing feedback
- delivering a structured evaluation

Evaluators should review speaker information on the website prior to the meeting.

---

# Pathways Progress Support

The VPE supports member progress through the Toastmasters education program.

Responsibilities include:

- helping members select projects
- answering Pathways questions
- encouraging speech completion
- recognizing educational achievements

Member development is the core mission of Toastmasters.

---

# Meeting Role Assignments

The VPE assigns meeting roles including:

| Role |
|-----|
Toastmaster |
Table Topics Master |
General Evaluator |
Timer |
Grammarian |
Ah-Counter |
Speech Evaluators |

Balanced role assignments help members gain experience.

---

# Agenda Publication

The meeting agenda should be finalized in advance whenever possible.

Recommended timeline:

| Timeline | Action |
|--------|-------|
3 weeks before | assign speakers |
1 week before | confirm roles |
24 hours before | finalize speech details |

Advance preparation improves meeting quality.

---

# Coordination with Toastmaster

Before each meeting the VPE should ensure the Toastmaster has:

- speaker names
- speech titles
- speaker introductions
- evaluator assignments

This helps the Toastmaster prepare smooth introductions.

---

# Recognition of Achievements

The VPE should recognize educational achievements such as:

- speech completions
- Pathways level completions
- milestone accomplishments

Recognition encourages member progress.

---

# Integration with Club Systems

The VPE works closely with several club systems:

- TM_Annual_Operating_Calendar_v1.0.md
- TM_Guest_Experience_System_v1.0.md
- TM_Table_Participation_System_v1.0.md
- TM_Agenda_Management_System_v1.0.md (future)

These systems help maintain meeting structure and member engagement.

---

# Common Failure Points

| Issue | Prevention |
|------|-------------|
Agenda incomplete | schedule roles early |
Speakers missing | confirm attendance before meeting |
Evaluators unprepared | encourage website review |
Uneven speaking opportunities | track member progress |

---

# Success Indicators

The VPE is successful when:

- meetings run smoothly
- speakers are well prepared
- evaluators provide strong feedback
- members progress through Pathways

---

# Officer Transition

At the end of the term the VPE should:

- transfer agenda management access
- share scheduling practices
- review the speaker pipeline

A smooth transition ensures continuity in the educational program.
