# Purpose

Speech contests are one of the most important annual traditions of the club.

They provide members an opportunity to:

- challenge themselves
- represent the club at higher contest levels
- demonstrate excellence in speaking
- experience formal Toastmasters competition

The club contest also strengthens meeting energy and attracts guests.

---

# Contest Cycles

Tulsa Club typically runs **two contest cycles per year**.

## Fall Contest Cycle (September)

Primary contest:

- Humorous Speech **or**
- Tall Tales

Secondary contest:

- Evaluation **or**
- Table Topics

This cycle focuses on **fun and engagement** early in the club year.

---

## Winter Contest Cycle (January)

Primary contest:

- International Speech Contest

Secondary contest:

- Evaluation **or**
- Table Topics

The International Speech Contest is the **most prestigious contest in Toastmasters** and may advance through:

- Area
- Division
- District
- Regional
- World Championship

---

# Contest Scheduling

Contests are normally scheduled for:

**The last Monday of the contest month**

Typical schedule:

| Month | Contest |
|------|--------|
September | Humorous or Tall Tales |
January | International Speech |

Secondary contests may vary depending on district requirements.

---

# Contest Leadership

The contest requires several key roles.

## Contest Chair

Responsible for:

- planning the contest
- coordinating contest officials
- ensuring rules compliance
- managing contest flow

The Contest Chair is usually:

- the President
- the Vice President Education
- or a designated member

---

## Contest Officials

The following roles are required.

| Role | Responsibility |
|----|----|
Chief Judge | oversees judging process |
Judges | evaluate contestants |
Timer | tracks speech timing |
Ballot Counter | collects and counts ballots |
Sergeant at Arms | manages contestant room and logistics |

Some roles may be combined depending on attendance.

---

# Planning Timeline

## 4 Weeks Before

Confirm:

- contest date
- contest types
- contest chair

Begin recruiting:

- contestants
- judges
- contest officials

Announce contest to members.

---

## 3 Weeks Before

Confirm contestants.

Recruit judges and contest officials.

Promote the contest through:

- club announcements
- club website
- social media

---

## 2 Weeks Before

Finalize:

- contest agenda
- contestant order
- judge assignments
- timer materials

Confirm contest rules.

---

## 1 Week Before

Confirm all roles.

Prepare:

- ballots
- timers
- speaking order draw
- certificates if used

# Contest Hospitality

Light refreshments are recommended.

Suggested items:

| Item | Quantity |
|-----|---------|
Fruit tray | 1 |
Vegetable tray | 1 |
Cookies | 1 tray |
Sparkling water | 1 case |
Bottled water | 1 case |

Estimated cost:

$50–$80.

Hospitality should not interfere with the formal tone of the contest.

---

# Room Setup

The venue uses **square tables with four chairs**.

Tables remain in their standard configuration.

Adjustments:

- create a **clear speaking area**
- position judges discreetly
- place hospitality tables at the rear

Suggested layout:

Speaker Area

Contestants Table

Audience Tables

Hospitality Area

---

# Contest Flow

A typical contest meeting structure:

| Segment | Time |
|--------|------|
Opening | 5 min |
Contest Briefing | 5 min |
Primary Contest | 30–40 min |
Break | 5 min |
Secondary Contest | 20–25 min |
Ballot Counting | 5 min |
Awards | 5 min |

Total time:

60–90 minutes.

---

# Contest Briefings

Two briefings must occur before the contest begins.

## Contestant Briefing

Contestants review:

- speaking order
- timing signals
- contest rules
- speaking area

---

## Judge Briefing

Judges review:

- judging criteria
- ballot procedures
- confidentiality rules

---

# Contest Rules

Contest rules are governed by Toastmasters International.

Key principles:

- fairness
- impartial judging
- confidentiality of judges
- consistent timing signals

Official rulebooks should be referenced for detailed requirements.

---

# Awards

At the conclusion of the contest:

Awards are presented for:

- First Place
- Second Place
- Third Place

Only the **First Place winner advances** to the next contest level.

Certificates or ribbons may be used.

---

# Guest Integration

Speech contests can attract guests.

Guests should:

- be welcomed at the start of the meeting
- receive a brief explanation of the contest
- be encouraged to attend future meetings

Contests are a strong recruitment opportunity.

---

# Promotion

Promote contests through:

- club website
- club announcements
- social media
- email reminders

Highlight the opportunity to watch **high-level speeches**.

---

# Post-Contest Responsibilities

After the contest:

- record winners
- notify area contest officials if necessary
- collect contest materials
- document lessons learned

Lessons learned should be recorded in:
TM_Playbook_Lessons_Learned_v1.0.md

# Common Failure Points

| Issue | Prevention |
|----|----|
Insufficient judges | recruit early |
Contest runs long | rehearse agenda |
Contestants unclear on rules | hold briefing |
Poor audience understanding | explain contest format |

---

# Success Indicators

A successful contest will:

- run smoothly
- follow contest rules
- showcase strong speeches
- create excitement within the club
- send a prepared contestant to the next contest level
