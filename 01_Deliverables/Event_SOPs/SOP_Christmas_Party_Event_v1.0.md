# Purpose

The Christmas Party serves as the Tulsa Club’s annual **holiday celebration and community gathering**.  

This meeting shifts the focus from formal educational programming to:

- member appreciation
- relationship building
- celebration of the year
- welcoming guests and family members

The event strengthens the club culture and closes the calendar year on a positive and relational note.

---

# Event Timing

The Tulsa Club Christmas Party is held on:

**Second Monday of December**

Rationale:

- avoids conflicts with holiday travel
- ensures the event occurs before the club shuts down for the final two weeks of December
- maintains predictable scheduling year to year

The club does **not meet the final two Mondays of December**.

---

# Event Objectives

The Christmas Party should accomplish the following goals:

1. Celebrate the accomplishments of the year
2. Strengthen relationships between members
3. Provide a relaxed and welcoming environment
4. Recognize member contributions
5. Welcome spouses, family members, and guests
6. Reinforce club community before the holiday break

# Hospitality Plan

The Christmas Party is the club’s **largest hospitality event of the year**.

Two formats are acceptable.

---

## Option 1 — Potluck Model

Members bring dishes to share.

Suggested categories:


| Category    | Examples               |
| ----------- | ---------------------- |
| Main dishes | casseroles, sliders    |
| Side dishes | vegetables, salads     |
| Desserts    | cookies, brownies      |
| Drinks      | sparkling water, cider |


Advantages:

- community participation
- variety of food
- lower cost

---

## Option 2 — Sam’s Club Hospitality Model

Food purchased using the club’s preferred vendor.

Suggested trays:


| Item            | Quantity |
| --------------- | -------- |
| Fruit tray      | 1        |
| Vegetable tray  | 1        |
| Cookie tray     | 1        |
| Sparkling water | 1 case   |
| Bottled water   | 1 case   |


Optional additions:

- holiday cupcakes
- seasonal desserts
- cider or hot chocolate

Estimated cost:

$50–$100 depending on selections.

# Room Setup

Venue uses **square tables seating four members**.

Tables should remain in their standard arrangement.

Adjustments:

- designate a **central speaking area**
- place food tables along the **side or rear wall**
- create space for casual conversation

Suggested layout:

Speaker / Activity Area

Table 1     Table 2

Table 3     Table 4

Hospitality Table(s)

Guest Sign-in

# Event Program Structure

The Christmas Party meeting is intentionally **less formal** than standard meetings.

Suggested structure:


| Segment                  | Time      |
| ------------------------ | --------- |
| Welcome                  | 5 min     |
| Dinner / Food            | 20–30 min |
| Member Recognition       | 10 min    |
| Holiday Speaking Segment | 10 min    |
| Games / Fun Activity     | 10–15 min |
| Closing Remarks          | 5 min     |


Total time:

60–75 minutes.

---

# Optional Activities

The following activities work well for holiday meetings:

### Holiday Table Topics

Examples:

- favorite Christmas memory
- best holiday gift ever received
- a tradition your family celebrates

---

### Holiday Stories

Members share short stories about:

- gratitude
- community
- personal growth

---

### Fun Activities

Examples:

- white elephant gift exchange
- holiday trivia
- storytelling round

Participation should remain voluntary.

---

# Member Recognition

The Christmas Party is a good time to acknowledge:

- officer contributions
- members completing speech pathways
- members who helped grow the club
- contest participants
- new members

Recognition should remain brief and sincere.

---

# Guest Integration

Guests and family members are welcome.

Guests should:

- be greeted warmly
- be introduced to members
- be invited to participate in holiday discussions

No guest should feel isolated at the event.

---

# Event Tone

The Christmas Party should feel:

- warm
- relaxed
- welcoming
- celebratory

It is not intended to be a formal Toastmasters educational meeting.

---

# Cleanup

Cleanup should be coordinated before the meeting ends.

Tasks:

- clear food tables
- collect trash
- return room to venue standards
- ensure all club materials are collected

Cleanup volunteers should be assigned in advance.

---

# Post-Event Notes

After the event, record:

- attendance
- guest participation
- food success or shortages
- improvements for next year

Document these notes in:

TM_Playbook_Lessons_Learned_v.md

# Common Failure Points

Avoid the following problems:

| Issue | Prevention |
|-----|-------------|
Too much structure | keep the event relaxed |
Too little food | confirm hospitality in advance |
Guests ignored | assign hosts |
Program runs too long | keep activities brief |

---

# Success Indicators

The Christmas Party is successful when:

- members stay and socialize
- guests feel welcomed
- members leave encouraged
- the club closes the year with strong morale

---
