# Purpose

The Tulsa Club Anniversary Meeting celebrates the founding and legacy of the club.

This event reinforces the club’s identity and history while strengthening the sense of community among current members, past members, and guests.

The anniversary meeting is designed to:

- celebrate the club’s longevity
- recognize past leadership
- invite alumni and former members
- highlight the club’s growth and accomplishments
- inspire current members to continue the legacy

---

# Event Timing

The Tulsa Club Anniversary Meeting is held on:

**The last Monday of October**

This timing allows the club to:

- celebrate its founding period
- invite former members during a stable part of the year
- avoid major holiday conflicts
- maintain a predictable annual rhythm

---

# Event Objectives

The anniversary meeting should achieve the following outcomes:

1. Celebrate the founding and legacy of the club
2. Recognize past officers and contributors
3. Strengthen community among members
4. Invite former members and alumni
5. Introduce guests to the club’s history and culture

# Event Program Structure

The anniversary meeting should be celebratory but structured.

Suggested agenda:

| Segment | Time |
|--------|------|
Welcome and Opening | 5 min |
Club History Reflection | 5–7 min |
Member Stories | 10 min |
Table Topics (Anniversary Theme) | 10 min |
Prepared Speech (Optional) | 5–7 min |
Recognition Segment | 5 min |
Closing Remarks | 3–5 min |

Total meeting length:

60–75 minutes.

---

# Anniversary Content Ideas

Possible content segments:

### Club History Reflection

Brief overview including:

- founding year
- significant milestones
- notable achievements

---

### Member Stories

Members share short stories about:

- how they joined the club
- personal growth experiences
- favorite moments in the club

---

### Anniversary Table Topics

Example prompts:

- What does this club mean to you?
- What is the most valuable lesson you learned here?
- Why do clubs like this matter?

---

### Recognition

Recognize:

- past officers
- long-time members
- members with significant contributions

Recognition should remain brief and sincere.

---

# Guest Integration

Guests and former members should be welcomed warmly.

Best practices:

- greet guests personally
- introduce them during the meeting
- invite them to share brief memories if comfortable

Former members often bring valuable historical perspective.

---

# Common Failure Points

| Issue | Prevention |
|------|-------------|
Too long program | keep reflections short |
Insufficient invitations | reach out early to alumni |
Overly formal tone | keep event celebratory |
No historical context | prepare club history notes |

---

# Success Indicators

The anniversary meeting is successful when:

- members feel proud of the club
- alumni reconnect with the club
- guests experience the club’s culture
- the event reinforces a sense of community and continuity
