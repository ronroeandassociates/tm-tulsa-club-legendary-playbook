# Purpose

The Open House is the club’s primary **membership growth event**.

Unlike a typical meeting, the Open House is designed specifically to:

- attract guests
- introduce Toastmasters in a clear and engaging format
- demonstrate the value of club membership
- encourage immediate membership applications

The event combines elements of a standard Toastmasters meeting with structured networking and interactive speaking opportunities.

---

# Event Timing

Open House events may occur:

- once or twice per year
- commonly in **March** or **early fall**

The event should be scheduled when the club can focus attention on guest recruitment.

---

# Event Objectives

The Open House should achieve the following outcomes:

1. Introduce guests to Toastmasters in a clear and engaging way
2. Allow guests to participate in speaking activities
3. Demonstrate the club’s culture and support environment
4. Encourage guests to join the club
5. Increase membership growth

---

# Event Leadership

Primary Event Lead:

**Vice President Membership**

Supporting roles:

- President
- Vice President Public Relations
- Vice President Education
- Sergeant at Arms
- Hospitality Lead

Each officer plays a role in guest experience and event promotion.

---

# Promotion Strategy

Promotion should begin **3–4 weeks before the event**.

Channels may include:

- club website
- social media
- email invitations
- personal invitations from members
- community event listings

Members should be encouraged to invite:

- friends
- coworkers
- family members
- community contacts

---

# Guest Registration

Guests should sign in upon arrival.

Information collected:

- name
- email
- phone number (optional)
- interest in communication or leadership development

A simple guest sign-in sheet is sufficient.

---

# Hospitality Plan

Open House events should provide **welcoming refreshments**.

Preferred vendor:

Sam’s Club.

Suggested items:

| Item | Quantity |
|------|---------|
Fruit tray | 1 |
Vegetable tray | 1 |
Cookie tray | 1 |
Sparkling water | 1 case |
Bottled water | 1 case |

Estimated cost:

$60–$100 depending on attendance.

Food should be available **before and after the meeting**.

---

# Room Setup

The venue uses **square tables with four chairs**.

Tables remain in their standard configuration.

Adjustments:

- create a clear speaking area
- position hospitality tables near the entrance
- place guest sign-in near the entrance
- display the Toastmasters banner prominently

Suggested layout:

Speaker Area

Table 1     Table 2

Table 3     Table 4

Hospitality / Guest Sign-in

---

# Open House Program Structure

The Open House should be **highly interactive**.

Suggested program:

| Segment | Time |
|--------|------|
Guest Welcome & Overview | 5 min |
Speak & Connect Networking | 10–15 min |
Table Topics (Guest Friendly) | 10–15 min |
Prepared Speeches | 10–15 min |
Evaluation Demonstration | 5–7 min |
Membership Invitation | 5 min |
Networking | 10 min |

Total program time:

60–75 minutes.

---

# Speak & Connect Segment

The Open House may include a **structured networking activity**.

Participants rotate through short conversations with prompts such as:

- introduce yourself
- share a recent accomplishment
- discuss communication goals

This activity helps guests:

- become comfortable speaking
- connect with multiple members
- experience supportive communication practice

---

# Table Topics

Guests should be invited (but not required) to participate.

Questions should be:

- simple
- friendly
- non-controversial

Examples:

- What is one skill you would like to improve?
- What inspired you to attend tonight?
- What is one goal you have this year?

---

# Prepared Speeches

If included, speeches should demonstrate:

- strong storytelling
- personal growth
- benefits of Toastmasters

Speakers should avoid overly technical projects.

---

# Evaluation Demonstration

A short evaluation should demonstrate how Toastmasters helps speakers improve.

Guests should understand that:

- feedback is supportive
- improvement is encouraged
- every member grows over time.

---

# Membership Invitation

The meeting should include a clear invitation to join.

The Vice President Membership or President may explain:

- membership benefits
- meeting schedule
- how to join
- next steps

Membership forms should be available.

---

# Guest Follow-Up

Guest follow-up should occur **within 24 hours**.

The Vice President Membership should send:

- thank-you message
- invitation to return
- membership information

Prompt follow-up increases conversion rates.

---

# Cleanup

Cleanup tasks include:

- clearing food tables
- disposing of trash
- returning room to venue standards
- collecting club materials

Cleanup volunteers should be assigned before the meeting ends.

---

# Post-Event Documentation

After the Open House record:

- number of guests
- number of membership inquiries
- number of new members
- lessons learned

Record insights in:

TM_Playbook_Lessons_Learned_v1.0.md

# Common Failure Points

| Issue | Prevention |
|------|-------------|
Low guest turnout | promote early and widely |
Guests feel lost | assign guest ambassadors |
Program too long | keep segments short |
No clear invitation | include membership pitch |

---

# Success Indicators

The Open House is successful when:

- guests feel welcomed and engaged
- guests participate in speaking activities
- guests return for future meetings
- membership inquiries increase

---

# Review and Update

This SOP should be reviewed annually during:

- membership planning
- officer transition
- after each Open House event

Adjustments should be based on guest feedback and membership growth results.
