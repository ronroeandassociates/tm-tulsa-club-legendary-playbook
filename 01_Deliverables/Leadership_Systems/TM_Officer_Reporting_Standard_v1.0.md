# TM_Officer_Reporting_Standard_v1.0

## Purpose

This standard defines **how club officers report during executive committee meetings**. The goal is to keep updates short, useful, and focused on the information the leadership team needs to make decisions.

Reports should highlight:

* key metrics
* notable progress
* risks or problems
* decisions needed

Reports should **not** repeat information already visible on dashboards unless clarification is required.

Target reporting time: **~60 seconds per officer**.

---

## Standard Officer Report Structure

Each officer update should follow this structure:

1. **Current Status**
2. **Progress Since Last Meeting**
3. **Risks or Issues**
4. **Requests / Decisions Needed**

Example format:

* "Speaker schedule is filled three weeks ahead."
* "Two guests attended last meeting and follow-ups were sent."
* "One member is pending dues renewal."

---

## Role-Specific Focus Areas

### Vice President Education

Focus on:

* speaker pipeline
* upcoming meeting readiness
* Pathways progress

Avoid detailed role assignments unless problems exist.

---

### Vice President Membership

Focus on:

* guest flow
* follow-ups
* membership conversions
* retention risks

---

### Vice President Public Relations

Focus on:

* marketing activity
* visibility in the community
* upcoming promotions or campaigns

---

### Treasurer

Focus on:

* current financial balance
* dues collection status
* upcoming financial obligations

---

### Secretary

Focus on:

* meeting minutes completed
* membership records updated
* administrative compliance

---

### Sergeant-at-Arms

Focus on:

* meeting setup readiness
* supplies and materials
* venue coordination

---

## Escalation Triggers

Officers should bring items to the executive team when:

* a problem requires leadership support
* a decision must be made
* an initiative requires coordination between officers

Routine operational updates should remain brief.

---

## Reporting Discipline

To maintain efficient meetings, the executive team follows these guidelines:

1. Officer updates remain under **60 seconds**.
2. Officers prepare updates **before the meeting**.
3. Discussion occurs **only when clarification or decisions are required**.

---

## Expected Outcome

When this reporting standard is followed, executive meetings will:

* remain focused
* reduce unnecessary discussion
* allow more time for strategic decisions

This reporting structure supports the **Leadership Operating System** defined in the Legendary Playbook.
