# Purpose

The Presiding Officer officially opens and closes the meeting.

This script provides a clear structure to ensure the meeting begins professionally, guests are welcomed, and control is smoothly transferred to the Toastmaster of the Day.

The script is a guide and should be delivered naturally rather than read verbatim.

---

# Before the Meeting

Arrive **15–20 minutes early**.

Tasks:

- greet members and guests
- confirm Toastmaster and meeting roles are present
- review announcements if needed
- coordinate briefly with the Sergeant at Arms and Toastmaster

A confident opening sets the tone for the meeting.

---

# Opening the Meeting

When the scheduled start time arrives:

"Good evening everyone.

I would like to call this meeting of Tulsa Club Toastmasters to order."

Pause briefly.

---

# Welcome Members and Guests

"We are very glad you are here this evening.

Toastmasters is a supportive environment where we practice communication and leadership skills."

If guests are present:

"Before we begin, we would like to recognize our guests."

Invite guests to briefly introduce themselves if comfortable.

Example:

"If you are visiting tonight, please feel free to share your name and how you heard about our club."

Keep this segment brief and welcoming.

---

# Brief Explanation for Guests

"For our guests, a typical Toastmasters meeting includes three parts:

• Table Topics, where we practice impromptu speaking  
• Prepared speeches, where members present projects  
• Evaluations, where speakers receive feedback to improve"

---

# Announcements (Optional)

Share brief announcements if necessary.

Examples:

- upcoming contests
- special events
- club milestones

Keep announcements **concise** so the meeting can move quickly into the program.

---

# Introduce the Toastmaster

Transition control of the meeting.

Example:

"Tonight’s program will be guided by our Toastmaster of the Day.

The Toastmaster serves as the host of the meeting and will guide us through the program."

Introduce the Toastmaster.

Example:

"Our Toastmaster this evening is [Name].

Please join me in welcoming [Name]."

---

# Return for Closing

After the evaluation portion concludes, the General Evaluator or Toastmaster returns control to the Presiding Officer.

Begin the closing.

---

# Closing the Meeting

"Thank you to everyone who participated in tonight’s meeting."

Recognize participants if desired:

- speakers
- evaluators
- guests

---

# Invite Guests to Return

Address guests directly.

"We are always glad to welcome visitors.

If you enjoyed tonight’s meeting, we invite you to join us again."

Encourage conversation after the meeting.

---

# Next Meeting Reminder

"Our next meeting will be held on [date]."

---

# Adjourn the Meeting

"This concludes tonight’s meeting of Tulsa Club Toastmasters.

Thank you all for attending."

---

# Key Principles

An effective Presiding Officer:

- starts the meeting on time  
- welcomes guests warmly  
- keeps announcements brief  
- transitions smoothly to the Toastmaster  
- closes the meeting professionally
