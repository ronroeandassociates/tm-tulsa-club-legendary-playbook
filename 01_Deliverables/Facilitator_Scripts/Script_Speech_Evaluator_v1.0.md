# Purpose

The Speech Evaluator provides constructive feedback to help the speaker improve their communication skills.

Evaluations are a core component of the Toastmasters educational experience. A strong evaluation highlights what worked well and provides specific suggestions for improvement.

The goal is to **encourage the speaker while helping them grow**.

---

# Preparation Before the Meeting

Before the meeting, evaluators should review the assigned speaker on the club website.

Information typically available includes:

- Path
- Level
- Project
- Speech Title
- Speaker Introduction

Evaluators should also review the **project evaluation resources** if available.

Preparation allows the evaluator to align feedback with the speaker’s learning objectives.

---

# During the Speech

While the speaker is presenting:

Take brief notes on:

- strengths of the speech
- areas for improvement
- memorable moments
- examples of effective communication

Focus on helping the speaker become better, not simply pointing out mistakes.

---

# Evaluation Timing

Typical timing signals are:

| Signal | Time |
|------|------|
Green | 2 minutes |
Yellow | 2 minutes 30 seconds |
Red | 3 minutes |

The evaluation should typically last **2–3 minutes**.

---

# When Called by the General Evaluator

Begin by addressing the speaker directly.

Example:

"Thank you, General Evaluator.

[Speaker Name], thank you for your speech titled '[Speech Title]'."

Acknowledge the speaker’s effort and presence.

---

# Highlight Strengths First

Begin with positive observations.

Example:

"What I appreciated most about your speech was..."

Examples of strengths to mention:

- strong opening
- clear structure
- engaging storytelling
- effective gestures
- vocal variety

Be specific so the speaker knows what worked well.

---

# Provide Suggestions for Improvement

Offer constructive suggestions.

Example:

"One suggestion that could strengthen your speech even further is..."

Suggestions should be:

- specific
- supportive
- actionable

Focus on one or two key improvement points.

---

# Reinforce the Message

Summarize the overall impact of the speech.

Example:

"Your message about persistence was clear and meaningful."

Encouragement is an important part of evaluation.

---

# Conclude the Evaluation

End with supportive encouragement.

Example:

"I look forward to seeing your continued progress in future speeches."

"Please join me in thanking [Speaker Name]."

---

# Key Principles

Effective evaluations:

- encourage the speaker
- provide clear observations
- suggest practical improvements
- maintain a positive tone

Evaluations should always support the speaker’s growth and confidence.
