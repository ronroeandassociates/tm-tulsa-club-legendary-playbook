# Purpose

This script provides a practical guide for the Toastmaster of the Day to facilitate the meeting program smoothly.

The Toastmaster acts as the **host of the meeting**, guiding the audience through each segment.

The script is not meant to be read word-for-word but provides structure for clear transitions.

---

# When Introduced

After the Presiding Officer opens the meeting, the Toastmaster is introduced.

Suggested response:

"Thank you, Madam/Mister President.

Good evening everyone and welcome to tonight’s meeting of Tulsa Club Toastmasters."

---

# Explain the Meeting Program

Briefly explain the structure for guests.

Example:

"For our guests this evening, Toastmasters meetings typically include three parts:

- Table Topics, where members practice impromptu speaking  
- Prepared speeches, where members present projects from the education program  
- Evaluations, where speakers receive feedback to help them improve"

---

# Introduce the Table Topics Master

Transition to Table Topics.

Example:

"One of the best ways to improve speaking skills is through impromptu speaking.

Our Table Topics Master tonight will lead this portion of the meeting."

Introduce the Table Topics Master.

---

# Transition to Prepared Speeches

After Table Topics concludes:

"Thank you for those excellent responses.

The next portion of our meeting is the prepared speech segment."

Explain that speakers are presenting Pathways projects.

---

# Introduce Speakers

Speaker introductions should include:

- name
- Path
- project level
- speech title

Example:

"Our first speaker tonight is Jane Smith.

Jane is working on Level 2 of the Presentation Mastery path.

Her speech this evening is titled 'Finding Confidence Through Challenge.'"

Invite the speaker.

"Please join me in welcoming Jane Smith."

---

# Transition to Evaluations

After all speeches:

"Prepared speeches help us practice communication.

Evaluation helps us improve."

Introduce the General Evaluator.

---

# Transition Back to Presiding Officer

At the end of the program:

"This concludes the program portion of our meeting.

I now return control of the meeting to our Presiding Officer."

---

# Key Principles

A successful Toastmaster:

- keeps the meeting flowing smoothly
- introduces speakers clearly
- maintains positive energy
- keeps transitions concise

The Toastmaster acts as the **host of the meeting experience**.
