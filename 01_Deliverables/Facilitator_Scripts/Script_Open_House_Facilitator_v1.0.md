# Purpose

The Open House Facilitator guides a special meeting designed to introduce guests to Toastmasters and encourage membership.

Unlike a regular meeting, the Open House focuses on:

- explaining how Toastmasters works
- demonstrating speaking and evaluation
- helping guests feel comfortable
- encouraging guests to return or join

The facilitator ensures the meeting is welcoming, engaging, and easy for guests to understand.

---

# Preparation Before the Event

Before the meeting, the facilitator should:

- review the Open House agenda
- confirm speaking roles
- coordinate with the VP Membership and VP Public Relations
- prepare brief explanations of Toastmasters segments
- ensure guest sign-in is ready

This preparation ensures the meeting runs smoothly.

---

# Opening the Open House

Example:

"Good evening everyone, and welcome to our Tulsa Club Toastmasters Open House."

"We’re very glad you are here tonight."

"For those who may be new to Toastmasters, this organization helps people improve communication and leadership skills in a supportive and positive environment."

---

# Explain the Meeting Structure

Briefly explain how the meeting works.

Example:

"Tonight you’ll experience several parts of a typical Toastmasters meeting."

"These include:

• Table Topics — short impromptu speaking  
• Prepared speeches — presentations by members  
• Evaluations — feedback to help speakers improve"

"This format helps members build confidence and communication skills."

---

# Encourage Guest Participation

Explain that guests may participate if they wish.

Example:

"If you’re visiting tonight, you’re welcome to simply observe."

"However, if you feel comfortable, you may also participate in our Table Topics segment."

Participation is always optional.

---

# Transition to Table Topics

Example:

"To begin, we will start with Table Topics."

"This portion allows members and guests to practice impromptu speaking."

Introduce the Table Topics Master.

---

# Prepared Speech Demonstration

After Table Topics, transition to prepared speeches.

Example:

"Now we will move to the prepared speech portion of the meeting."

"Members deliver prepared speeches as part of the Toastmasters education program."

Introduce the speaker(s).

---

# Explain Evaluations

Before the evaluation portion, explain its purpose.

Example:

"One of the most valuable parts of Toastmasters is evaluation."

"Speakers receive constructive feedback that helps them improve."

Introduce the General Evaluator.

---

# Membership Invitation

Toward the end of the meeting, briefly explain how guests can join.

Example:

"If you enjoyed tonight’s meeting, we would love to have you become part of our club."

"Membership provides the opportunity to practice speaking regularly, receive feedback, and develop leadership skills."

"Club members will be available after the meeting to answer any questions."

---

# Encourage Conversation After the Meeting

Example:

"We invite you to stay for a few minutes after the meeting to talk with members and learn more about Toastmasters."

"This is a great time to ask questions and meet other members."

---

# Close the Open House

Example:

"Thank you all for joining us this evening."

"We hope you enjoyed experiencing how Toastmasters helps people grow as communicators and leaders."

---

# Key Principles

A successful Open House facilitator:

- keeps explanations simple and clear  
- focuses on guest experience  
- encourages participation without pressure  
- maintains an enthusiastic and welcoming tone  
- highlights the benefits of membership
