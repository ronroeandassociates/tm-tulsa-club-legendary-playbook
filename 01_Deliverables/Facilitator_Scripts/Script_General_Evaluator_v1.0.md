# Purpose

The General Evaluator leads the evaluation portion of the meeting.

This role oversees the evaluation team and ensures that speakers and meeting participants receive helpful feedback.

The General Evaluator also evaluates the **overall quality of the meeting**, identifying strengths and opportunities for improvement.

---

# Preparation Before the Meeting

Before the meeting, the General Evaluator should:

- review the meeting agenda
- identify the assigned evaluators
- confirm the Timer, Grammarian, and Ah-Counter are present
- briefly coordinate expectations with the evaluation team

This preparation ensures the evaluation segment runs smoothly.

---

# When Introduced by the Toastmaster

Begin with a brief explanation of the evaluation portion for guests.

Example:

"Thank you, Toastmaster.

The evaluation portion of the meeting is where we provide feedback to help speakers and participants improve."

"In Toastmasters, evaluation is one of the most valuable parts of the learning process."

---

# Introduce Speech Evaluators

Introduce each evaluator before they give their report.

Example:

"Our first evaluator is [Evaluator Name], who will evaluate [Speaker Name]."

After the evaluation is completed, thank the evaluator and move to the next one.

Example:

"Thank you, [Evaluator Name], for that thoughtful evaluation."

---

# Invite Supporting Role Reports

After the speech evaluations, invite the remaining evaluation team members.

## Timer Report

Example:

"I now invite our Timer to provide the timing report."

---

## Grammarian Report

Example:

"I now invite our Grammarian to share their report."

---

## Ah-Counter Report

Example:

"I now invite our Ah-Counter to provide their report."

---

# Evaluate the Meeting

After the reports, provide a brief evaluation of the meeting overall.

Focus on:

- meeting organization
- effectiveness of transitions
- energy and engagement
- helpful suggestions for improvement

Example:

"Overall, tonight’s meeting had strong energy and engagement."

"One suggestion that could improve future meetings is..."

Keep this evaluation concise and constructive.

---

# Conclude the Evaluation Segment

Summarize the importance of feedback.

Example:

"Evaluations help us improve and grow as communicators."

"Thank you to everyone who contributed to tonight’s meeting."

---

# Return Control to the Presiding Officer

Conclude with a clear transition.

Example:

"I now return control of the meeting to our Presiding Officer."

---

# Key Principles

An effective General Evaluator:

- encourages helpful feedback
- keeps the evaluation segment organized
- recognizes strong meeting elements
- provides constructive suggestions
- maintains a positive and supportive tone
