# Purpose

The Grammarian helps improve the club’s use of language.

This role encourages members to become more aware of their grammar, vocabulary, and word choice during the meeting.

The Grammarian also introduces a **Word of the Day**, encouraging members to incorporate it into their speaking.

---

# Preparation Before the Meeting

Before the meeting begins, the Grammarian should:

- select a **Word of the Day**
- prepare a brief definition
- prepare an example sentence
- review the meeting agenda

The Word of the Day should:

- be easy to pronounce
- be useful in everyday conversation
- encourage members to expand their vocabulary

Example:

Word of the Day: **Articulate**  
Definition: able to express ideas clearly and effectively  
Example sentence: "A good speaker is able to present ideas in an articulate and engaging manner."

---

# When Introduced by the Toastmaster

When called upon early in the meeting, explain the role and introduce the Word of the Day.

Example:

"Thank you, Toastmaster.

As Grammarian, my role is to listen carefully to how language is used throughout the meeting."

"I will note examples of effective language, as well as any opportunities to improve grammar or word choice."

---

# Introduce the Word of the Day

Explain the Word of the Day clearly.

Example:

"Tonight’s Word of the Day is **Articulate**."

"It means the ability to express ideas clearly and effectively."

"A sample sentence might be: 'She delivered an articulate presentation that kept the audience engaged.'"

"I encourage everyone to try using this word during the meeting."

---

# During the Meeting

While participants speak, the Grammarian should listen for:

- effective word usage
- strong vocabulary
- creative phrasing
- grammar errors if they occur
- usage of the Word of the Day

The Grammarian should take brief notes during the meeting.

---

# Grammarian Report

During the evaluation portion of the meeting, the General Evaluator will invite the Grammarian to give their report.

Example introduction:

"Thank you, General Evaluator.

During tonight’s meeting I observed several examples of effective language."

---

# Share Observations

Mention a few positive examples of language use.

Example:

"I appreciated the vivid language used in Jane’s speech when she described overcoming a challenge."

If necessary, briefly mention grammar improvements.

Example:

"There were also a few minor grammar opportunities such as subject-verb agreement."

Keep the report supportive and concise.

---

# Word of the Day Report

Report whether the Word of the Day was used.

Example:

"The Word of the Day was **Articulate**, and it was used twice during Table Topics."

---

# Conclude the Report

End with encouragement.

Example:

"Improving our use of language helps us become clearer and more effective communicators."

"Thank you."

---

# Key Principles

An effective Grammarian:

- promotes strong language use
- encourages vocabulary development
- observes grammar patterns
- delivers feedback constructively

The goal is to help members become more confident and articulate speakers.
