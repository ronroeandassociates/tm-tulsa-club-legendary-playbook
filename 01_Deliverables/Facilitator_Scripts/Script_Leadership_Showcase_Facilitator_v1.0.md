# Purpose

The Leadership Showcase meeting highlights leadership opportunities within the club and provides a platform for recognizing and inducting the incoming officer team.

This meeting celebrates member growth and encourages members to take on leadership roles within the club.

The facilitator guides the meeting through leadership reflections, officer recognition, and the induction ceremony.

---

# Preparation Before the Meeting

Before the meeting, the facilitator should:

- review the meeting agenda
- confirm the incoming officer list
- coordinate with the Presiding Officer
- prepare brief remarks on leadership in Toastmasters
- confirm who will administer the officer induction (often the Area Director or a past club officer)

This preparation ensures the leadership transition is presented clearly and professionally.

---

# Opening the Leadership Showcase Segment

When introduced by the Toastmaster or Presiding Officer:

Example:

"Thank you.

Tonight we are celebrating one of the most important parts of Toastmasters — leadership."

"Toastmasters is not only about developing speaking skills, but also about developing leaders."

"This evening we recognize members who are stepping forward to guide our club in the coming year."

---

# Brief Leadership Reflection

Provide a short reflection on leadership in Toastmasters.

Example:

"Leadership in Toastmasters is learned by doing."

"Members grow as leaders by taking roles, organizing meetings, mentoring others, and serving as officers."

"These experiences help members develop confidence, organization skills, and the ability to lead teams."

---

# Recognize the Current Officer Team

Thank the outgoing officer team.

Example:

"Before we welcome our new officers, we want to recognize the dedication of the officers who have served this past year."

Invite applause for the outgoing team.

Optional:

Briefly mention key accomplishments from the year.

---

# Introduce the Incoming Officer Team

Introduce each incoming officer.

Example:

"Our incoming officer team for the new Toastmasters year is as follows:"

| Role | Name |
|-----|------|
President | [Name] |
VP Education | [Name] |
VP Membership | [Name] |
VP Public Relations | [Name] |
Secretary | [Name] |
Treasurer | [Name] |
Sergeant at Arms | [Name] |

Ask the incoming officers to stand or come forward.

---

# Officer Induction Ceremony

If another person is conducting the induction (Area Director or Past President), transition to them.

Example:

"It is now my pleasure to invite [Name] to administer the officer induction."

If the facilitator is conducting the induction, proceed with the oath.

Example:

"Please raise your right hand and repeat after me."

"I pledge to fulfill the duties of my office to the best of my ability."

"I pledge to support the members of this club in their development."

"I pledge to help maintain a positive and supportive learning environment."

---

# Welcome the New Officers

After the induction:

"Please join me in congratulating our new officer team."

Allow time for applause.

---

# Leadership Encouragement

Encourage members to pursue leadership opportunities.

Example:

"Leadership in Toastmasters is not limited to officer roles."

"Every meeting role is an opportunity to practice leadership."

"Each of us grows when we step forward to serve."

---

# Transition Back to the Meeting

Return control to the Toastmaster or Presiding Officer.

Example:

"Thank you for joining us in celebrating leadership within our club."

"I now return control of the meeting to our Toastmaster."

---

# Key Principles

A successful Leadership Showcase facilitator:

- celebrates member leadership
- recognizes service contributions
- presents the officer transition clearly
- encourages future leaders
- maintains an inspiring tone
