# Purpose

The Timer ensures that all meeting segments stay within their allotted time.

Timing is important because it helps meetings run efficiently and allows each participant to receive their full speaking opportunity.

The Timer also provides speakers with visual signals indicating their progress within the time limits.

---

# Preparation Before the Meeting

Before the meeting begins, the Timer should:

- obtain the timing device or timing cards
- review the agenda for timing ranges
- confirm timing signals for each segment
- test the timing equipment if necessary

Common timing tools include colored cards or digital timers.

---

# Timing Signals

Timing signals are typically displayed using colored cards.

| Color | Meaning |
|------|---------|
Green | minimum speaking time reached |
Yellow | approaching maximum time |
Red | maximum time reached |

Speakers should aim to conclude shortly after the green signal and before the red signal.

---

# Typical Timing Ranges

Timing varies depending on the meeting segment.

### Table Topics

| Signal | Time |
|------|------|
Green | 1 minute |
Yellow | 1 minute 30 seconds |
Red | 2 minutes |

---

### Prepared Speeches

Prepared speech timing depends on the specific Pathways project.

However, many speeches follow this general pattern:

| Signal | Time |
|------|------|
Green | 5 minutes |
Yellow | 6 minutes |
Red | 7 minutes |

The Toastmaster will confirm the exact timing range before the speech begins.

---

### Speech Evaluations

| Signal | Time |
|------|------|
Green | 2 minutes |
Yellow | 2 minutes 30 seconds |
Red | 3 minutes |

---

# During the Meeting

The Timer should:

- start timing when the speaker begins speaking
- display timing signals clearly
- record each speaker’s time
- remain attentive throughout the meeting

The Timer should avoid distracting the speaker while displaying signals.

---

# Recording Times

The Timer records the total speaking time for:

- Table Topics speakers
- prepared speakers
- speech evaluators

These times will be reported during the evaluation portion of the meeting.

---

# Timer Report

When called by the General Evaluator, deliver the timing report.

Example:

"Thank you, General Evaluator.

Here are the timing results for tonight’s meeting."

Then report the times for each segment.

Example:

Table Topics:

- John Smith — 1 minute 42 seconds  
- Emily Carter — 1 minute 58 seconds

Prepared Speeches:

- Jane Doe — 6 minutes 12 seconds  
- Mark Lee — 6 minutes 45 seconds

Evaluations:

- Sarah Kim — 2 minutes 30 seconds

Keep the report brief and clear.

---

# Key Principles

An effective Timer:

- signals clearly
- records times accurately
- remains attentive throughout the meeting
- helps maintain meeting schedule

Timing discipline helps the entire meeting run smoothly.
