# Purpose

The Table Topics Master leads the impromptu speaking portion of the meeting.

This segment allows members and guests to practice thinking on their feet and speaking without preparation.

The goal is to create an engaging, supportive environment where participants feel comfortable speaking.

---

# Preparation Before the Meeting

Prepare **4–6 questions or prompts** related to a theme.

Prompts should:

- be clear and easy to understand
- encourage storytelling or opinions
- avoid controversial or uncomfortable topics
- allow participants to speak for 1–2 minutes

Example themes:

- personal growth
- communication experiences
- leadership lessons
- everyday life situations

Bring the questions written down or on note cards.

---

# When Introduced

When the Toastmaster introduces you, begin with a brief explanation.

Example:

"Thank you, [Toastmaster Name].

One of the best ways to improve speaking skills is to practice speaking without preparation."

"For our guests, this portion of the meeting is called **Table Topics**."

"In Table Topics, I will ask a question and invite someone to respond with a short speech."

---

# Explain the Timing

Briefly explain the timing signals.

Example:

"Our Timer will signal:

• Green at 1 minute  
• Yellow at 1 minute 30 seconds  
• Red at 2 minutes"

"The goal is to speak for about one to two minutes."

---

# Invite Participation

Encourage participation from members and guests.

Example:

"I will first invite members who do not already have a meeting role."

"If any guests would like to participate, you are very welcome, but participation is optional."

---

# Conduct Table Topics

Ask a question, then call on a participant.

Example:

"Our first question is:

'What is a skill you would like to improve this year, and why?'"

Pause briefly.

"[Participant Name], would you like to respond?"

After the participant finishes, thank them and move to the next question.

Example transition:

"Thank you for that great response."

"Our next question is…"

---

# Manage Time

Typically allow **4–6 participants** depending on time.

Keep responses moving and avoid long explanations between speakers.

Maintain positive energy.

---

# Conclude Table Topics

After the final speaker:

"Thank you to everyone who participated in Table Topics tonight."

"This portion of the meeting helps us practice thinking quickly and expressing our ideas clearly."

---

# Return Control to the Toastmaster

End with a smooth transition.

Example:

"I now return control of the meeting to our Toastmaster."

---

# Key Principles

A successful Table Topics Master:

- prepares engaging questions  
- keeps the segment moving  
- encourages participation  
- creates a supportive atmosphere  
- respects time limits  

The goal is to make impromptu speaking enjoyable and educational.
