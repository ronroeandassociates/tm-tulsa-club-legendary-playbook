# Purpose

The meeting closing provides a professional conclusion to the Toastmasters meeting.

This segment reinforces the positive atmosphere of the meeting, recognizes participants, and invites guests to return.

The closing should be brief, encouraging, and organized.

---

# When the Closing Occurs

The closing occurs after:

- speech evaluations
- Timer report
- Grammarian report
- Ah-Counter report
- General Evaluator summary

At this point the meeting program is complete and control has returned to the Presiding Officer.

---

# Begin the Closing

Example opening:

"Thank you to everyone who participated in tonight’s meeting."

"Toastmasters works because members support one another in developing communication and leadership skills."

Pause briefly.

---

# Recognize Participants

Recognize key contributors.

Possible acknowledgments include:

- prepared speakers
- speech evaluators
- meeting role participants
- guests who participated in Table Topics

Example:

"Let’s thank our speakers and evaluators for their excellent contributions tonight."

---

# Recognize Guests

Guests should always be acknowledged.

Example:

"We are especially grateful to our guests for joining us this evening."

"If you enjoyed tonight’s meeting, we would be delighted to see you again."

Encourage guests to stay after the meeting for conversation.

---

# Announce Next Meeting

Provide the next meeting date.

Example:

"Our next meeting will be held on [date] at [location]."

"We invite everyone to return and continue developing their communication and leadership skills."

---

# Optional Announcements

If necessary, brief reminders may be included:

- upcoming speech contests
- open house meetings
- special club events

Announcements should remain concise.

---

# Final Encouragement

End with a positive message.

Example:

"Thank you all for helping create another supportive and engaging meeting."

---

# Adjourn the Meeting

Officially conclude the meeting.

Example:

"This concludes tonight’s meeting of Tulsa Club Toastmasters."

"Have a great evening."

---

# Key Principles

A successful closing:

- recognizes participants
- thanks guests
- reinforces the club’s welcoming culture
- ends the meeting on time
- invites continued engagement
