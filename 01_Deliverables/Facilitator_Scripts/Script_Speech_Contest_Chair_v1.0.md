# Purpose

The Speech Contest Chair serves as the host and facilitator of the Toastmasters speech contest.

This role ensures the contest runs according to Toastmasters rules and maintains a professional, organized, and fair environment for contestants and judges.

The Speech Contest Chair introduces the contest, manages transitions, and ensures the audience understands the process.

---

# Preparation Before the Contest

Before the contest begins, the Chair should confirm that all contest roles are filled.

Typical contest roles include:

- Chief Judge
- Contest Toastmaster (optional)
- Timer
- Ballot Counters
- Sergeant at Arms
- Contestants

The Chair should also confirm:

- speaking order
- timing signals
- contest eligibility
- judging process

Preparation helps the contest run smoothly.

---

# Opening the Contest

Example opening remarks:

"Good evening and welcome to the Tulsa Club Toastmasters Speech Contest."

"Toastmasters speech contests provide members an opportunity to showcase their speaking skills while advancing through the Toastmasters contest program."

"Tonight’s contest will follow Toastmasters International contest rules."

---

# Introduce Contest Type

Example:

"Tonight we will conduct the [Contest Name] contest."

Examples include:

- International Speech Contest
- Evaluation Contest
- Table Topics Contest
- Humorous Speech Contest
- Tall Tales Contest

Briefly explain the purpose of the contest.

---

# Introduce Contest Officials

Introduce key officials.

Example:

"I would like to introduce our Chief Judge, who will oversee the judging process."

"The identities of the judges will remain confidential to ensure fairness."

Recognize the Timer and Ballot Counters.

---

# Explain Contest Procedures

Provide a short explanation of how the contest will proceed.

Example:

"The speaking order has been randomly determined."

"Each contestant will be introduced by the Contest Toastmaster."

"Contestants will speak within the designated time limits."

Explain the timing signals if appropriate.

---

# Introduce Contest Toastmaster

If a Contest Toastmaster is used:

Example:

"To guide us through the speaking portion of the contest, please welcome our Contest Toastmaster."

The Contest Toastmaster will introduce each contestant.

---

# During the Contest

The Speech Contest Chair monitors the contest to ensure:

- procedures are followed
- transitions are smooth
- timing signals are visible
- judges have adequate time to complete ballots

The Chair should remain attentive but unobtrusive.

---

# Ballot Collection

After all contestants have spoken:

Example:

"Ballot counters, please collect the judges’ ballots."

Pause while ballots are collected.

The Chief Judge verifies results.

---

# Recognition of Contestants

Before announcing results, thank the contestants.

Example:

"Please join me in recognizing the effort and courage of all our contestants."

Allow audience applause.

---

# Announce Contest Results

Results are announced starting with third place, if applicable.

Example:

"Third place goes to..."

"Second place goes to..."

"And the winner of tonight’s contest is..."

Allow applause after each announcement.

---

# Closing the Contest

Example:

"Congratulations to all of our contestants and thank you to everyone who helped make tonight’s contest possible."

"The winner will advance to the next level of the Toastmasters contest."

---

# Key Principles

An effective Speech Contest Chair:

- maintains fairness and professionalism
- follows official contest rules
- keeps the program organized
- recognizes all participants
- celebrates the accomplishments of contestants
