# Purpose

The Ah-Counter helps speakers become aware of filler words and verbal habits that may distract from their message.

By tracking these verbal pauses and repeated phrases, the Ah-Counter helps members improve clarity and confidence in their speaking.

The goal is not criticism, but awareness and improvement.

---

# Preparation Before the Meeting

Before the meeting begins, the Ah-Counter should:

- review the meeting agenda
- prepare a sheet for recording observations
- listen carefully to all speakers during the meeting

The Ah-Counter should remain attentive during all speaking portions of the meeting.

---

# What the Ah-Counter Tracks

The Ah-Counter listens for filler words and repeated sounds.

Common examples include:

| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
Filler words | ah, um, uh |
Repeated phrases | you know, like, so |
Verbal pauses | well, okay, basically |
Habit words | repeated phrases used unconsciously |

These habits often appear when a speaker is thinking about what to say next.

---

# During the Meeting

The Ah-Counter should:

- listen to each speaker
- record filler words when they occur
- note frequent patterns

Speakers to observe include:

- Table Topics participants
- prepared speakers
- speech evaluators
- meeting facilitators

The Ah-Counter should remain discreet while taking notes.

---

# When Called to Report

During the evaluation portion of the meeting, the General Evaluator will invite the Ah-Counter to deliver their report.

Example introduction:

"Thank you, General Evaluator.

The role of the Ah-Counter is to help us become aware of filler words and verbal habits."

---

# Delivering the Report

Provide a brief summary of observations.

Example:

"During tonight’s meeting I noticed several common filler words such as 'um', 'ah', and 'you know'."

If helpful, provide a few examples:

"John used 'um' three times during Table Topics, and Sarah used 'you know' twice during her evaluation."

Avoid over-detailing every occurrence.

The goal is awareness rather than criticism.

---

# Conclude the Report

End with encouragement.

Example:

"Becoming aware of these verbal habits helps us become more confident and effective speakers."

"Thank you."

---

# Key Principles

An effective Ah-Counter:

- listens carefully
- tracks filler words consistently
- reports observations constructively
- maintains a supportive tone

The purpose of the role is to help members improve clarity and confidence in their speaking.
