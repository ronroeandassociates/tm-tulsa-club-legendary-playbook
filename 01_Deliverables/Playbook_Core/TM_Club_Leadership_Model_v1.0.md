# TM_Club_Leadership_Model_v1.0

## Purpose

This document defines the leadership philosophy, structure, and operating expectations for club officers and emerging leaders.

The objective is to ensure the club develops capable leaders while maintaining stability, clarity of responsibility, and operational continuity across officer transitions.

Leadership within the club is viewed not as status, but as stewardship of the member experience and the health of the club.

---

# 1. Leadership Philosophy

Leadership within the club is built on four principles:

1. Stewardship of the club
2. Service to members
3. Development of future leaders
4. Consistent operational execution

Officers are responsible for ensuring the club functions smoothly while creating opportunities for others to grow.

Leadership is not defined by authority but by responsibility for the success of the environment.

---

# 2. Leadership Structure

The club operates through a distributed leadership model consisting of elected officers and active members.

### Officer Roles

Typical officer roles include:

- President
- Vice President Education
- Vice President Membership
- Vice President Public Relations
- Secretary
- Treasurer
- Sergeant at Arms
- Immediate Past President (advisory)

Each role has clearly defined operational responsibilities.

The officer team functions collectively as the **Club Executive Committee**.

---

# 3. Officer Responsibilities

Officers are responsible for maintaining the systems that sustain the club.

Core officer responsibilities include:

- Maintaining meeting quality
- Supporting member development
- Managing club administration
- Supporting club growth
- Maintaining communication
- Preparing future leaders

Officers should understand both the tasks and the purpose behind their role.

---

# 4. President — Strategic Leadership

The President serves as the strategic and cultural leader of the club.

Primary responsibilities include:

- Setting tone and standards for the club
- Leading officer meetings
- Ensuring officer coordination
- Supporting officer effectiveness
- Maintaining focus on the club mission
- Representing the club within the district

The President ensures alignment between systems, culture, and mission.

---

# 5. Vice President Education — Program Leadership

The Vice President Education oversees the educational experience of members.

Key responsibilities include:

- Meeting agenda structure
- Role assignments
- Speech scheduling
- Pathways progress awareness
- Evaluator preparation
- Meeting quality

This role directly influences the quality of the member development experience.

---

# 6. Vice President Membership — Member Experience

The Vice President Membership focuses on member growth and guest integration.

Responsibilities include:

- Guest follow-up
- Membership conversion
- New member onboarding
- Member retention
- Supporting welcoming culture

This role ensures the club remains healthy and growing.

---

# 7. Vice President Public Relations — Visibility

The Vice President Public Relations manages external communication and awareness.

Responsibilities include:

- Club promotion
- Website updates
- Social media
- Event announcements
- Guest outreach messaging

The goal is to ensure the club remains visible and accessible to prospective members.

---

# 8. Secretary — Documentation

The Secretary maintains institutional memory and records.

Responsibilities include:

- Meeting minutes
- Officer records
- Club documentation
- Historical records
- Administrative tracking

Accurate records support continuity during leadership transitions.

---

# 9. Treasurer — Financial Stewardship

The Treasurer ensures financial stability and transparency.

Responsibilities include:

- Dues management
- Financial reporting
- Budget awareness
- Membership payments
- District financial requirements

Financial clarity supports sustainable club operation.

---

# 10. Sergeant at Arms — Meeting Environment

The Sergeant at Arms manages the meeting environment and logistics.

Responsibilities include:

- Room setup
- Timing equipment
- Ballots and materials
- Guest greeting support
- Meeting logistics

A well-prepared meeting environment contributes to a professional experience.

---

# 11. Leadership Collaboration

The officer team functions as a collaborative leadership unit.

Officers should:

- communicate regularly
- support each other's roles
- address problems early
- share information
- maintain alignment on priorities

Leadership effectiveness depends on cooperation rather than isolated effort.

---

# 12. Leadership Development Pipeline

A healthy club intentionally develops future leaders.

Leadership development opportunities include:

- meeting roles
- contest organization
- mentoring relationships
- committee work
- project leadership
- officer shadowing

Developing future leaders ensures long-term club stability.

---

# 13. Officer Preparation and Handover

Leadership continuity depends on effective officer transitions.

Outgoing officers should provide:

- documentation of systems
- key contacts
- recurring responsibilities
- timelines and calendars
- lessons learned

Incoming officers should receive orientation and support.

---

# 14. Decision-Making Principles

Leadership decisions should follow consistent principles.

Decisions should prioritize:

1. Member development
2. Meeting quality
3. Club sustainability
4. Cultural health

Short-term convenience should not undermine long-term club strength.

---

# 15. Leadership Conduct

Club leaders should model the behaviors expected of members.

Leadership conduct includes:

- preparation
- reliability
- respectful communication
- encouragement
- accountability

Leadership behavior establishes the tone of the club.

---

# 16. Leadership Visibility

Effective leaders remain visible and accessible.

Leaders should:

- engage with members regularly
- support new members
- attend meetings consistently
- assist with roles when needed
- communicate openly

Leadership presence reinforces trust within the club.

---

# 17. Leadership Evaluation

Officers should periodically evaluate their effectiveness.

Areas for reflection include:

- meeting quality
- member participation
- guest experience
- leadership pipeline
- communication effectiveness

Reflection enables continuous improvement.

---

# 18. Shared Ownership of Club Success

While officers coordinate systems, the club's success depends on member participation.

Members support leadership by:

- fulfilling meeting roles
- encouraging participation
- mentoring newer members
- contributing to club initiatives

Leadership succeeds when responsibility is shared.

---

# Closing Principle

Leadership within the club exists to serve the growth of members and the strength of the club.

When leaders focus on:

- mission alignment
- system reliability
- member development
- cultural integrity

the club becomes a stable environment where people consistently grow as communicators and leaders.
