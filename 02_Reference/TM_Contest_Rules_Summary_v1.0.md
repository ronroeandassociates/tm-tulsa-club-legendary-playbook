# TM_Contest_Rules_Summary_v1.0

## Purpose

This document provides a simplified summary of Toastmasters speech contest rules for reference during club planning and contest preparation.

Official rules are governed by Toastmasters International and should always be consulted for final authority.

---

# Common Contest Types

Toastmasters contests commonly include:

International Speech Contest

Evaluation Contest

Table Topics Contest

Humorous Speech Contest

Tall Tales Contest

Not all contests are held every year. Contest cycles vary depending on Toastmasters International guidelines.

---

# Contest Progression

Contests typically advance through several levels:

Club Contest  
Area Contest  
Division Contest  
District Contest

Winners from each level advance to the next level.

---

# Contest Eligibility

Contestants generally must:

• be active members in good standing  
• have completed required speech prerequisites for certain contests  
• represent only one club per contest type

Specific eligibility requirements vary by contest.

---

# Contest Roles

Typical contest roles include:

Contest Chair  
Chief Judge  
Judges  
Timers  
Ballot Counters  
Sergeant at Arms

These roles help ensure fairness and consistency.

---

# Judging Criteria

Judges evaluate speeches using criteria such as:

speech development

organization

delivery

language use

speech effectiveness

Specific scoring guidelines are defined by Toastmasters International.

---

# Timing Rules

Each contest type has defined timing requirements.

Timers typically provide signals indicating:

minimum time

target time

maximum time

Exceeding the allowed time may result in disqualification.

---

# Contest Environment

Contest environments should be:

organized

fair

consistent

supportive

Professional execution reflects positively on the club and district.
